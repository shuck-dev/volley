# Art Direction

The creative direction for Volley!, distilled into themes. This doc translates the feel we want into rules the [Art Bible](bible.md) can formalise, and it explains why specific works in the [Inspirations](inspirations.md) list matter.

This layer is narrative. The bible is the decision layer.

---

## Generous presentation, honest underneath

The presentation is generous: saturated colour, interior light, hand-drawn confidence. Underneath, harder material runs honest. The art holds both at once; neither is the joke.

## Simple shapes, full emotion

Characters read through silhouette and body language, not through detail. A few well-chosen shapes should feel more expressive than a finely drawn model. Postures, pauses, and small gestures carry more weight than faces.

## Hand-drawn, committed

The line has life in it. Confident, slightly imperfect, visibly made by someone. The game lives in the line quality as much as in the colours. No flat vector polish.

## Motion as character

Personality lives in movement. How a character shifts weight, how they land, how they wait. Fewer frames, better chosen. The animation carries the mood before the face does.

## Rhythm in everything

Hits, misses, rallies, streaks, reactions, UI feedback all land on a felt rhythm. The game has a pulse the art and sound both serve.

## Two styles, same world

The same characters, the same court, the same rules, rendered two ways. The art needs to hold Construction's generous presentation and Reality's quieter weight without either reading as a betrayal of the other.

## Diegetic, specific, lived-in

UI, props, and interfaces belong to the world. The ball rack, the gear rack, the shop, the shipment mat are physical objects the main character uses. Everything the player touches is something that could exist in the fiction.

## Looked at, not copied

Influences are honest. The game takes from a wide field: sports animation, cozy film, games with twin styles, painterly environments, rhythm-aware composition. None of it is transplanted; each is translated into Volley!'s own palette, line, and tempo.
