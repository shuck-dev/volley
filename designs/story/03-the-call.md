# The call

On the other side, the path is short. Grass and rosemary, a low wall at the cliff, a bench against the wall. The bench has a plaque on the back of it. The plaque says Adrian's name.

The shopkeeper is on the bench, smaller than the protagonist remembers. They are wearing a shirt the protagonist has never seen. They are holding a phone. The wind is coming from the sea.

The protagonist stops a few feet from the bench. They take out their own phone and open the call log. The number sits where it has always sat: row fifteen, no contact name. The number that is the world record. The number the championship match landed on. The shopkeeper's number, all this time. The protagonist presses it.

The phone in the shopkeeper's hand rings.

The shopkeeper looks at the screen. They have been letting it ring for months. They could let this one ring. The shopkeeper looks up.

The protagonist is on the path. Their phone is at their ear. Their hand is shaking.

The shopkeeper looks at the protagonist for a long time.

The shopkeeper picks up.

---

The line opens. Neither of them speaks. They are looking at each other across the grass. The line stays open.

The protagonist crosses the grass and sits on the bench. The shopkeeper does not move. The phone is between them, in two hands, the line open. The protagonist hears their own breath through it.

"Hi," the protagonist says, into the phone, with the shopkeeper a foot away.

The shopkeeper says, "Hi."

After a while the shopkeeper puts the phone down between them, screen up, the call still running. They look at the sea. The protagonist puts their phone down beside the shopkeeper's. They look at the sea too. The line stays open.

---

The credits play over the two of them on the same court. The rally between them is uneven. The shopkeeper has lost a step. The protagonist is rustier than they would have admitted. The rally goes long anyway. The friend at the counter is folding a rag.

The cliff is behind them. The bench is there. The number in the protagonist's phone has a contact name now.

The gate stays open.
