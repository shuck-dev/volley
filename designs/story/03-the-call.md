# The call

On the other side, the path is short. Grass and rosemary, a low wall at the cliff, a bench against the wall. The bench has a plaque on the back of it. The plaque says Adrian's name.

Zach is on the bench, smaller than the protagonist remembers. He is wearing a shirt the protagonist has never seen. He is holding a phone. The wind is coming from the sea.

The protagonist stops a few feet from the bench. They take out their own phone and open the call log. The number sits where it has always sat: row fifteen, no contact name. The number that is the world record. The number the championship match landed on. Zach's number, all this time. The protagonist presses it.

The phone in Zach's hand rings.

Zach looks at the screen. He has been letting it ring for months. He could let this one ring. He looks up.

The protagonist is on the path. Their phone is at their ear. Their hand is shaking.

Zach looks at the protagonist for a long time.

Zach picks up.

---

The line opens. Neither of them speaks. They are looking at each other across the grass. The line stays open.

The protagonist crosses the grass and sits on the bench. Zach does not move. The phone is between them, in two hands, the line open. The protagonist hears their own breath through it.

"Hi," the protagonist says, into the phone, with Zach a foot away.

Zach says, "Hi."

After a while Zach puts the phone down between them, screen up, the call still running. He looks at the sea. The protagonist puts their phone down beside his. They look at the sea too. The line stays open.

---

The credits play over the two of them on the same court. The rally between them is uneven. Zach has lost a step. The protagonist is rustier than they would have admitted. The rally goes long anyway. The friend at the counter is folding a rag.

The cliff is behind them. The bench is there. The number in the protagonist's phone has Zach's name on it now.

The gate stays open.
