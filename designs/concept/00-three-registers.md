# The Three Registers

The high-level structure of Volley as a game. This doc carries the architecture; per-arc docs in this folder carry the detail.

This is a working set of docs. Refinement passes follow.

## What the game is

The protagonist holds a racquet. Their stated goal is to beat the world volley record. Friends help them along the way. The count climbs, the rally tiers climb with it, the friends keep showing up. The hook arrives in the friend's voice in the first session, not on a HUD; the count gives a number, the friend gives the number meaning.

What the player does not know yet: the world record is a phone number. The shopkeeper's. Every volley counts toward dialling someone the protagonist could reach but won't. The construction is a coping shape; reaching the record means making the call.

Construction is what the protagonist actively maintains. It takes energy to keep. The warmth in it is real; the pretense is the rendering. The game is the arc of that pretense thinning, the wall coming down, and the protagonist learning to live without it.

### Two worlds, layered allusion

Construction and Reality are distinct worlds, two registers, two maps, two visual treatments. The architecture lands in two layers of allusion the player meets in order. The first session presents Construction as the world; the player is alluded to the premise that this is where the protagonist lives. As cracks form across the climb, the second allusion lands: Construction is a construct, not the real world the protagonist lives in. The garden the player has been rallying in is built from the protagonist's memory of their actual garden in Reality, sprucing it up. The constructed garden and the real garden remain separate places; the architecture does not collapse them into one.

### Pre-smartphone era

The game world is set in a pre-smartphone era, late 90s or early 2000s as a tonal range. Phones are flip, candy-bar, or land-line. Phone numbers are held in heads or on paper, not auto-named in pocket databases. The period shapes art direction (clothes, signage, phone hardware), tone, and one load-bearing mechanic: the unnamed number.

### The unnamed number

The shopkeeper's phone number sits in the protagonist's phone, in the call log or contacts list, unnamed. Period-appropriate: numbers without auto-attached names. The protagonist either deleted the contact, never saved the name, or the device just holds digits without labels. They scroll past it sometimes; they know whose it is and they do not.

The player has full control of the phone throughout the game. They can dial the unnamed number any time, in Construction or Reconstruction, before the cliff. It never connects. The shopkeeper has withdrawn and is not picking up. The unanswered ring is the player-facing manifestation of the protagonist's reach without contact.

The number recognition is also the count. The championship-count digits the player has been climbing toward in Construction are the same digits sitting unnamed in their phone. The protagonist does not see the match across Part 1; the player may. The recognition tightens across Part 2 as fragments surface in Reality. By late Reconstruction the protagonist knows whose it is. The dial at the cliff is the moment the name attaches.

## The three registers

**Construction.** Saturated, full, helping. Volleyball lives here; the count is the engine; the world record is the goal. The protagonist's pretense actively maintained against a loss they have not yet been able to face. Detail in `01-construction.md`.

**Reality.** A complete second register. Visually distinct from Construction: weighted, naturalistic, plainer, characters at their actual ages. Story-driven; layered scenes the player walks into. One place, the protagonist's hometown, geographically static with content added across the arc. Detail in `04-reality.md`.

**Reconstruction.** The arc. The long phase after the break in which the wall stays down and the player can travel between Construction and Reality at will. Not a third visual register; a meta-state of free travel and bidirectional carry. Reconciliation lives inside Reconstruction as the actions the player takes. Detail in `03-reconstruction.md`.

## The two parts

The game divides into Part 1 and Part 2. The seam is structural; the player may not perceive it as a chapter break, but the architecture splits there.

**Part 1: Construction.**

1. **Construction.** The pretense actively maintained. The rally climbs toward the championship. The count is visible; the goal is named in dialogue. The protagonist's obsession with becoming the champ deepens across the climb, pulling in two directions at once: become the champion of the world they built, and become the champ inside that world (their dead friend, drawn as the partner at the final venue). (Detail: `01-construction.md`.)
2. **Cracks.** Reality leaks into the construction in small atmospheric ways. Each crack is dismissible; the cumulative pressure matters. Tonal and meta-contextual, both deniable. (Detail: `02-cracks-and-break.md`.)
3. **The break.** The player wins the championship at the final venue and beats the champ. The win feels off: the construct's central goal has been reached and proved meaningless, both pulls of becoming-champ satisfied and neither was the thing. The win is the rupture. At the win moment the count completes; the digits land; the protagonist sees them and they look weirdly familiar, matching the unnamed number in their phone, but the recognition does not quite land yet. The construct cannot hold itself together once its goal has been hollowed out, and the player is pulled into Reality involuntarily for the first time. The walk through the protagonist's hometown reveals that the shopkeeper is missing: the shop is empty, the family worried. The person the protagonist was reaching for without knowing has actually disappeared. The champ exits at this point. (Detail: `02-cracks-and-break.md`.)

**Part 2: Reconstruction.**

4. **Reconstruction.** The driving force is the search for what happened, shadowed by dread. The protagonist believes the shopkeeper has followed the friend's path; calls to the unnamed number do not connect; each reconciliation action is also a piece of a question whose answer might be terrible. Free travel between registers; bidirectional carry; the volley becomes memory recall, each rally surfacing a memory that becomes a photo and points to a place in Reality. Coaches share their own memories of the shopkeeper as the player rallies with them. Across the arc the protagonist almost-dials the unnamed number repeatedly; the championship-count digits start surfacing in Reality where they should not be (on the back of a found photo, etched on the closed shop's sign, scrawled in the workshop, half-spoken by a coach), and the number is becoming a phone number across the player's eyes. The album fills, the hidden compartment opens, the sister hands over the key. (Detail: `03-reconstruction.md`.)
5. **The call.** The player returns to the garden with the key, unlocks the one locked gate, walks through to the cliff. The shopkeeper is on a bench dedicated to the friend, alive. The Part 2 dread inverts to relief. The protagonist dials the unnamed number, the digits whole now; the shopkeeper's phone rings beside them on the bench; they could ignore it the way they have been ignoring all the others, and they do not. Both hold their phones across the small distance between path and bench. The dial bridges intention, not space, and finally attaches the name to the number. After the call the protagonist crosses, sits beside the shopkeeper. Coming home is the call. (Detail: `05-postgame.md`.)

After the call: postgame. The shopkeeper joins as the championship-spot partner; the cliff stays returnable through the now-unlocked gate.

## Cross-register principles

These principles bind the registers together; the per-arc docs should not contradict them.

- **Everyone is real.** The supporting cast in Construction is the real cast from the protagonist's life, rendered young / vibrant / helping. The same people exist in Reality at their actual ages. Two asset sets per character that appears in both. Exception: the champ (Construction-only; their reality is the cliff).
- **The garden is the meeting point.** The garden is the one venue grounded in the protagonist's actual hometown geography. Other Construction venues (underwater, meteor, treetop canopy, etc.) are purely fantastic, with no Reality counterparts. The garden carries the construct-and-real overlap on its own. Its "out of place out of time" feel comes from being the venue that is built from a memory of a real place still standing.
- **One locked gate.** There is one locked gate in the entire game, at the back of the garden in Construction. Walking through it transitions to Reality; the cliff is on the other side, in Reality's hometown geography. The single-gate canon makes the key's destination obvious when the key arrives.
- **The hook is in dialogue, not HUD.** The world record is named by a character (probably the friend at the stall) in the first session. The number is on a HUD; the meaning is not.
- **Cracks are tonal, not concrete.** A real-world object literally appearing in Construction reads as a flag the player can point at. A colour shift or a beat in a partner's posture is harder to name and easier to absorb. Concrete leakage breaks the deniability the cumulative shape needs.
- **Reconstruction is not a third visual register.** The two registers stay distinct. Reconstruction's signal is the carry, plus a gradual weathering of Construction's saturation, plus play-level differentiators (score hidden in Construction, champ dialogue softens, audio shifts).
- **Reality is finite hand-crafted content.** One place, built once, with iterative additions across Reconstruction. Reality cannot be procedural; the team builds each scene.

## What this teaches each surface

- **Artist world bible**: two asset sets per character that appears in both registers (champ is the exception). Reconstruction's visual signal is the carry plus weathering, not a third render. Cracks are tonal and atmospheric; do not draw concrete reality-leaks.
- **MC profile**: the protagonist's reconciliation arc is the spine of their interior life. The call-of-the-void layer the profile already names becomes legible as the resistance to crossing into Reality, with becoming-the-champ and the unnamed-number layer as sibling interiors (the obsession pulling toward title and dead friend at once; the unsaved contact in the phone the protagonist scrolls past, dials, and finally hears answered). The cliff is where that resistance is met head-on, and the call there is chosen presence rather than a closing of distance.
- **Tech-context**: the rally tooling holds Construction. Reality's tooling is its own thing: layered scene-state, contextual interactions, dialogue, descriptive prose, the present-and-attentive puzzle shape, the bidirectional carry. SH-279 is the right place to spike this.
- **Audio (SH-281)**: a register per phase. Construction is synthetic / bright / energetic; Break is abstract synth, harsh, minimalist; Reality is acoustic with bustle and wind; Reconstruction is synth and acoustic in conversation, escalating to full orchestra by the end.

## What this teaches the production

- Construction's content scales (procedural rally, item economy, partner system).
- Reality's content does not. The hometown is built once with iterative additions across Reconstruction. The cliff is a separate location built once. Finite hand-crafted content.
- Reconstruction's length is a budget knob: more reconciliation actions means longer arc but more content to build.
- Idle pacing means the cracks need to escalate slowly enough that month-long players see them as cumulative rather than clustered around early sessions.
- The cast doubles in Reconstruction (each supporting-cast character with a Reality-side asset). Worth bounding the partner count early.

## What we are picking up from the prior narrative pass

Strong keeps from `designs/02-alpha/01-world-and-narrative.md`:

- The world record is the shopkeeper's phone number. The hook resolves into the call.
- Peace is the call made.
- The shopkeeper is the friend who got pushed away. Same person as the friend at the stall. Two renders.
- The champ is the dead friend. Construction only. Returns as a partner once the cliff is faced.
- The hometown is the reality place. Coastal, British-seaside-meets-southern-Spain.

Reframings:

- The shopkeeper's role in the death has shifted from "tried to help after" to "involved but not responsible." Something else caused it; the shopkeeper was present, entangled, a daily reminder of the failure. This sharpens why the protagonist blocks them out and why the call is hard.
- The post-break mechanic in the prior doc was badge activation (slotting narrative items into milestone badges). The new architecture replaces this with the bidirectional carry. Badge activation may persist as one expression of the carry inside Construction, or be dropped entirely. SH-279 will decide.
- The narrative doc framed the post-break phase as a single arc through denial-to-acceptance. The new architecture splits it into Reconstruction (the arc) and Reconciliation (the actions inside it).
