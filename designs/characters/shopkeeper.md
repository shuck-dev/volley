# The shopkeeper

Zach. The friend at the stall in Construction. The person the protagonist pushed away in Reality.

The art bible at [`../art/bible.md`](../art/bible.md) § 4 names the body and the visual role; this profile holds the relationship and the arc.

---

## Who they are

Survivor. Zach was on the cliff with the friend the day the friend died; the protagonist was not. Two survivors of the same routine, one of them at the edge when it stopped being routine. That asymmetry is the wedge.

Zach carries the memory; the protagonist carries the absence. Talking would mean naming where the protagonist was instead. The protagonist pushes them away because Zach IS the failure of presence, made daily and visible. Not as judgment; as mirror.

---

## Across the arc

**Construction** (Part 1). Present at the stall in every venue up to the break. The warm centre Construction is built around. With Zach the garden is a place; without him it is a fenced patch of grass with a ball in the air.

**The break.** The shop closes. Stephanie is at the counter, crying. Zach is gone. No one has heard from him. The friend at the stall is gone from Construction; the warm centre is hollowed out.

**Reconstruction** (Part 2). The protagonist believes Zach has followed the friend's path back to the cliff. He exists only in Reality across this arc, withdrawn.

**The cliff and the gate.** The final memory yields a key; the key unlocks the gate at the back of the garden. The protagonist steps through to the cliff. Zach is on the memorial bench, alive. The dread inverts to relief.

**Postgame.** Holds the right side of the court that used to be the championship spot, as the actual partner rather than the substitute.

---

## Open questions

- Gender, age, presentation. Deliberately open. The anchor is the relationship to the protagonist and the family resemblance to Stephanie.
- Where Zach has been across the search. The cliff at the end is the place the gate has been locked since the friend died; the protagonist has not thought to check there because the gate has been locked their whole life. How Zach reached the cliff, and how he passed the time there, is unresolved.
