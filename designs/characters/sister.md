# The sister (the tinkerer)

The shopkeeper's younger sister. The tinkerer in Construction; the photo-book holder in Reality. The bridge across Reconstruction.

The art bible at [`../art/bible.md`](../art/bible.md) § 4 names the body and the visual role; this profile holds the relationship and the arc.

---

## Who they are

Less weighted by the death than the shopkeeper. The protagonist can sit with her; she is willing to do the work the shopkeeper cannot yet do alongside them. She is the bridge.

In Construction she rests, procrastinates, works at her own pace; finished items land on a done tray with a soft chime. Different rhythm from the shopkeeper, different relationship to the protagonist, same family ease. She talks less than the shopkeeper, hands things back when they are done.

In Reality she holds the photo book. She is one of the first reachable people across Reconstruction; sitting with her and the book is the first reconciliation action.

---

## Across the arc

**Construction** (Part 1). The tinkerer behind the workshop bench in the garden. Reachable for the levelling, destruction, and synergy work that supports the rally.

**Reconstruction** (Part 2). Holds the half-filled photo album with the hidden compartment. The protagonist sits with her first, then goes to find the scattered photos. She receives each one as it returns. She mentions things in passing as the album fills: the way her brother used to leave the shop door, what was on the radio that morning, a song he played too loud.

**The cliff and the call.** When the album fills, the hidden compartment opens. The key is inside. She hands it to the protagonist.

**Postgame.** Still there.
