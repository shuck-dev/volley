# The champ

The dead friend, rendered into Construction as the championship final. Construction-only; their Reality is the cliff.

The art bible at [`../art/bible.md`](../art/bible.md) § 4 names the body and the visual role; this profile holds the relationship and the arc.

---

## Who they are

The friend the protagonist used to play with. The one who was the best at the game and pushed the protagonist to be better. The player the protagonist always admired, painted at championship scale because that is the size the friendship sits at in the protagonist's chest.

The friend died years ago in a cliff-jumping accident. Cliff jumping was a normalised activity in the friend group, the kind of thing they did on a hot afternoon and would do again next week, until the day it stopped being routine. The cliff in Reality is the actual cliff. The shopkeeper was there and the protagonist was not, and that arithmetic is the shape the rest of the game is bent around.

---

## Across the arc

**Construction** (Part 1). Holds the championship spot at the top of the tournament ladder. Present-in-absence across the climb, the spot the protagonist is climbing toward; appears as the partner only at the final venue, on the right side of the court for the championship match itself.

**The break.** The championship match is winnable. The protagonist beats the champ. The win lands wrong, and the win IS the break. The champ exits at this point and does not return.

**Reconstruction** (Part 2). Absent. The championship spot stands empty.

**The cliff and the call.** The cliff in Reality is the place where they died. The bench is dedicated to them.

**Postgame.** The championship spot is filled by the shopkeeper, as the actual person rather than the substitute.

---

## The doubled meaning

The same word does both jobs. "Becoming the champ" is winning the title and reaching the dead friend, in one motion. The protagonist does not separate them. Both pulls satisfy at the same beat at the championship win, and neither was the thing the protagonist actually needed.
