## 1. CS internship pipeline collapse

### 1a. Handshake — postings down, applications up

**Claim:** Internship postings on Handshake fell more than 15% between January 2023 and January 2025, while applications surged. Software engineering, formerly a top-five role for graduating seniors, fell to ninth on the platform in 2024-25.

**Source:** Handshake, *Internships Index 2025*, joinhandshake.com, published 2025 (survey ran through January 2025; 6,000+ students/recent grads). https://joinhandshake.com/network-trends/handshake-internships-index-2025/

**Direct figures:**
- "Internship postings on Handshake declined by more than 15% between January 2023 and January 2025."
- "The average applications per internship skyrocketed to 109 for 2024-2025, compared to 62 in 2023-2024 and 43 in 2022-2023." (Tech roles: 273 applications per posting — most competitive of any sector.)

### 1b. Handshake — CS-major-specific data

**Claim:** Software engineer roles on Handshake dropped from the single most-posted senior role for the Class of 2020 to ninth most-posted for 2024-25. Seventy percent of Class of 2026 CS majors describe themselves as "at least somewhat pessimistic" about their careers.

**Source:** Handshake Network Trends, *Class of 2026 Spotlight: Computer Science Majors*, 14 August 2025. https://joinhandshake.com/research/economic-research/class-of-2026-spotlight-computer-science-majors/

**Direct figures:**
- "Software engineer roles fell from top 5 (2018-2023) to 9th most-posted on Handshake (2024-2025)."
- "70% of Class of 2026 CS majors report being at least somewhat pessimistic about careers; 14% express optimism."
- "64% of pessimistic CS majors cite generative AI as a career concern factor."
- "~30% would have chosen different majors had they foreseen GenAI development."

### 1c. NACE — full-time offer/conversion rates collapse

**Claim:** US employers extended full-time offers to 62% of their 2024 intern class, down from ~67% the year before — the lowest offer rate in five years.

**Source:** National Association of Colleges and Employers (NACE), *2025 Internship & Co-op Report*, March 2025. Summary article: "Intern Conversion Rate Fell, Fueled by Lower Offer Rate." https://www.naceweb.org/talent-acquisition/trends-and-predictions/intern-conversion-rate-fell-fueled-by-lower-offer-rate (executive summary PDF: https://www.naceweb.org/docs/default-source/default-document-library/2025/publication/executive-summary/2025-nace-internship-and-coop-report-executive-summary.pdf — 403 to bots; cited via NACE's own summary articles).

**Direct figures:**
- "On average, employers extended offers of full-time employment to 62% of their 2024 intern class; in comparison, they extended offers to two-thirds of their 2023 interns. The current offer rate is the lowest in five years."
- 2022-23 cohort conversion: ~53%, down from ~58% the prior year (NACE 2024 report).
- Survey window: 16 October 2024 – 2 January 2025; 247 organisations.

### 1d. Big Tech intern programmes cut

**Claim:** Major tech intern classes were openly cut starting summer 2023 and never recovered to pre-2022 sizes.

**Source:** Fortune, "Big Tech is shrinking its intern classes," 13 May 2023. https://fortune.com/2023/05/13/google-meta-amazon-tech-companies-cutting-back-summer-internships/

**Direct figures (each attributed to company spokespeople):**
- **Nvidia:** 1,500+ interns in 2022 → ~600 in 2023 (a ~60% cut).
- **Amazon:** ~18,000 interns in 2022 → "reducing the number of interns for 2023 by more than half" (NYT, December 2022).
- **Google/Alphabet:** "more than 3,500 interns" in 2021 → "fewer interns this summer than in previous years" (no disclosed 2023 figure).
- **Meta:** "fewer interns this summer than last year," in line with announced workforce reductions.

(Note: more recent Big-Tech-specific intern-class numbers for 2024 and 2025 are not publicly reported by these companies. The Handshake 15% postings drop and NACE 5-year low together are the best aggregate signal for 2024-25.)

## 2. Co-op programmes — the gold-standard pipelines are wobbling

### 2a. University of Waterloo — 22-point spring 2025 collapse

**Claim:** Waterloo's co-op employment rate — historically 90%+ — fell to 77.3% in spring 2025, the worst since records began (previous low was 86.5% during COVID).

**Source:** Tiffany Wen, "UW's co-op program sees a significant unemployment rate for spring 2025," *The Imprint* (University of Waterloo student paper), 21 May 2025. https://uwimprint.ca/uws-co-op-program-sees-a-significant-unemployment-rate-for-spring-2025/

Cross-reference for normal-year baseline: Waterloo's official Co-operative Education employment statistics page, which reports fall 2024 overall employment of 96.8%. https://uwaterloo.ca/co-operative-education/about-co-op/employment-statistics

**Direct figures:**
- Spring 2025 overall: 77.3%; Engineering 78.8% (highest); Health 71% (lowest).
- Spring 2024 overall: 93%. Year-over-year drop: ~22 percentage points.
- 2,149 students still seeking work as of 21 May 2025.
- Previous record low: spring 2020 (COVID), 86.5%.

### 2b. Northeastern — 100-application cap as a rationing mechanism

**Claim:** Northeastern University, whose co-op programme is the canonical US example of a CS degree wired to industry placements, capped student applications at 100 per cycle on its NUWorks platform in fall 2024 — explicitly because the CS market shrank.

**Source:** Mara Macri, "Northeastern implements a 100 co-op application limit on NUWorks," *The Huntington News* (Northeastern student paper), fall 2024. https://huntnewsnu.com/80415/campus/northeastern-implements-a-100-co-op-application-limit-on-nuworks/

**Direct quote:** Elizabeth Zulick, Senior Vice Chancellor for Education Innovation at Northeastern, attributed the cap and the broader squeeze to the fact that "the economy, outside Northeastern, we're seeing more layoffs in these areas," and described the university "trying to shift the mindset around co-op applications and working on job development and growth internally."

**Stat:** As of 9 October 2024, only 601 NUWorks postings returned for Khoury College of Computer Sciences students using the "computer science" search term.

## 3. Capstone / industry-sponsored project programmes hollowing out

### 3a. University of Sydney — industry partners walked

**Claim:** Sydney's flagship CS capstone, which once placed students with external companies, is now overwhelmingly internal: only 9 of 43 projects in 2025 have an external industry partner; 34 are run by university faculty.

**Source:** David Braue, "Syd Uni capstone shake-up raises red flags," *Information Age* (Australian Computer Society), 23 February 2026. https://ia.acs.org.au/article/2026/-syd-uni-capstone-shake-up-raises-red-flags-.html

**Direct figures and quotes:**
- 2017: 28 capstone projects with external industry partners.
- 2025: 9 external-partner projects out of 43 total; 34 run internally.
- Cian Byrne (former industry partner): "They're telling me 'there's no one here' and no real industry partners involved." On the skills gap of his last student cohort: "It was surprising and shocking how much worse [his last students] were... they're just missing out."

(This is one institution, but a useful representative of the global trend: industry partners pulling out of capstone sponsorship as headcount budgets tighten.)

### 3b. Counter-evidence (worth noting for honesty)

University of Washington's industry capstone programme grew slightly in 2024-25: 65 sponsors, 96 projects (up from 54 sponsors, 95 projects in 2023-24). https://www.engr.washington.edu/industry/capstone-projects/2024-2025 — UW remains a healthy outlier; the Sydney pattern is not yet universal.

## 4. Faculty / dean-level acknowledgement of the pipeline break

### 4a. Hany Farid (UC Berkeley) — "Five offers down to one"

**Claim:** A senior CS faculty member at UC Berkeley, on the record, says the internship-and-offer pipeline that defined his programme for years has collapsed.

**Sources:**
- Brent D. Griffiths, *Business Insider*, "A leading computer-science professor says 'something is brewing' as graduates struggle to find work," 28 September 2025. (Mirrored at https://www.yahoo.com/news/articles/leading-computer-science-professor-says-095502798.html)
- UC Berkeley School of Information faculty news, "Computer Science Students Are Struggling to Get Jobs, Prof. Hany Farid Tells Business Insider," 28 September 2025. https://www.ischool.berkeley.edu/news/2025/computer-science-students-are-struggling-get-jobs-prof-hany-farid-tells-business-insider

**Direct quotes from Farid:**
- "Our students typically had five internship offers throughout their first four years of college. They would graduate with exceedingly high salaries, multiple offers." [pause] "They're happy to get one job offer."
- "Computer science went from a future-proof career to an industry in upheaval in a shockingly small amount of time."
- "Something is happening in the industry. I think it's a confluence of many things. I think AI is part of it. I think there's a thinning of the ranks that's happening, that's part of it, but something is brewing..."

### 4b. Magdalena Balazinska (Director, Paul G. Allen School of CS, University of Washington)

**Claim:** Even at one of the strongest US CS programmes, the dean now publicly frames the curriculum's job is to prepare students to keep moving as the field changes — not to deliver them into stable entry-level roles.

**Source:** Clare Duffy and Lisa Eadicicco, "150 job applications, rescinded offers: Computer science grads are struggling to find work," *CNN Business*, 28 August 2025. https://edition.cnn.com/2025/08/28/tech/computer-science-graduates-job-hunt-ai

**Direct quote:** "Our philosophy has always been that computer science is a field that advances faster than any other field, so preparing students to advance with the field is the most important thing we can do." — Magdalena Balazinska, University of Washington.

(The same CNN piece confirms three of the essay's already-cited stats with primary attribution: Oxford Economics May 2025 — CS/math grad employment down 8% since 2022; Indeed/St Louis Fed — software-development postings down 71% Feb 2022 to Aug 2025; NY Fed — 6.1% unemployment for recent CS grads, 7.5% for computer engineering.)
