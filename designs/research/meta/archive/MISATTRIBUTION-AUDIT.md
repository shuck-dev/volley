Scope: every named figure plus attached claim, stat, or quote across sections 01–14 and the bibliography in section-15-sources.md. Pattern being hunted: a primary source (researcher, analyst, company) whose work has been relabelled under a popularizer (writer, blogger, podcaster) in the text.

### [Section 01 — Jim Butcher "bear" quote]
- **Current text:** "His preferred line is one he borrows from Jim Butcher: 'You don't have to run faster than the bear to get away. You just have to run faster than the guy next to you.'"
- **Cited as:** [\[8\]](#ref-8) — Chris Zukowski, "How many wishlists should I have when I launch my game?"
- **Issue:** Low-risk but worth flagging. The quip pre-dates Butcher — a near-identical wolf variant appears in *The Mammoth Book of Zingers, Quips, and One-Liners* (2004), and versions of the joke circulate as folk humour well before Butcher's novels. Butcher used a version in print, which is how it enters quote databases, and Zukowski has reliably attributed the line to Butcher in his own writing. So the essay's chain (Zukowski → Butcher) is consistent with how Zukowski cites it; the deeper origin is older folk wisdom that Butcher popularised in a novel. Not a misattribution per the essay's stated chain, but the Butcher credit is a popularizer credit, not a primary one.
- **Correct attribution:** Jim Butcher popularised a pre-existing joke; the anonymous older form ("you don't have to outrun the bear, just outrun your friend") is the true origin.
- **Proposed fix:** Optional. Either leave as-is (Zukowski's own attribution) or soften to "a line popularised by Jim Butcher". Low priority.
