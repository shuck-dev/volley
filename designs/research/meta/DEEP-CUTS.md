# Deep-cuts review

The essay at 12,755 words is defensible but at the upper limit of what most readers will commit to. This document identifies which parts pay their weight and which do not, with the goal of letting the author choose a target length deliberately.

## Three target lengths and what each requires

### Target: 10,000 words (~22% cut, "tight")

Goal: remove ~2,755 words of genuine repetition and ancillary beats without reopening any seam the critiques closed.

- **Section 03, Double Fine Adventure opening paragraph (~140 words).** The Tim Schafer / 2 Player Productions origin is restated in full in section 04 when PsychOdyssey arrives. Either cut the section 03 opener and start on Team Cherry, or cut the section 04 restatement. The cleaner cut is here; Team Cherry's Adelaide-and-Newgrounds opener is stronger as the Part II lead.
- **Section 04, Double Fine PsychOdyssey stretch (~420 words, paragraphs covering Amnesia Fortnight, Massive Chalice, Psychonauts 2, the Microsoft acquisition, PsychOdyssey premiere, and the "layoffs happen on camera" beat).** The devlog section already carries Bay 12, Barone, and Freehold. PsychOdyssey earns one tight paragraph (acquisition, 33 episodes, the layoffs on camera), not five. CRITIQUE flagged Double Fine as the weakest case against an acquisition-cadence rebuttal; keep the caveat-aware minimum.
- **Section 07, Buffer / Levels / Indie Hackers cluster (~300 words).** Three non-games examples where one suffices. Buffer is the sharpest (the 1,263-to-2,886 number lands); Levels.io and Indie Hackers overlap with it on the "public revenue" point. Cut the Levels and Allen paragraphs; keep Buffer and Linux.
- **Section 07, Benkler / Shirky / Doctorow theory graf (~110 words).** Three theorists, one sentence each, back-to-back. The essay's argument does not need its theoretical pedigree flagged; the cases carry the load. Cut the whole paragraph; lose nothing the reader needs.
- **Section 08, Copilot / Cursor / Benioff / Zuckerberg stack (~180 words).** The proximate-cause paragraph. The Stanford and NY Fed figures earlier in the section already carry the displacement claim. Cut Benioff and Zuckerberg quotes; keep one Copilot/Cursor sentence. The quotes read as op-ed flourish against the paper citations that preceded them.
- **Section 09, GitHub Octoverse / Outreachy paragraph (~120 words).** The globally-distributed-contributors beat is a detour from the accessibility spine of the section. It arrived to support "broad participation" but the Celeste / TLOU2 / Xbox Adaptive Controller / Forza cases already do that work. Cut.
- **Section 10, Valve origin stories stretch (~260 words).** The Counter-Strike / Team Fortress / Portal / Garry's Mod / Dota paragraph is an important beat but over-stuffed. Compress to two cases (Counter-Strike and Portal) plus the "almost every Valve franchise" summary line. The other three are redundant evidence for the same claim.
- **Section 10, Valve Handbook / flatland paragraph (~160 words).** The most-litigated Valve case (People Make Games, PC Gamer, stack-ranking) opens more seams than it closes. CRITIQUE flagged Valve as already the section's weakest link. The Handbook paragraph adds a defensiveness the essay does not need. Cut.
- **Section 11, FLI letter / Bletchley / Seoul / Paris paragraph (~130 words).** The summit roll-call. One sentence naming "a series of partial international commitments from Bletchley to Paris" covers it. The dates add little the paragraph needs.
- **Section 12, Eleanor Roosevelt quote paragraph (~120 words).** Lovely, but it sits between the "claim is structural, not guaranteed" line and the close. Cutting it tightens the transition and loses a beat the section does not require.
- **Cross-essay micro-cuts (~500 words).** Redundant half-paragraphs: section 05's Celeste sales closing line; section 06's Godot-Unity-controversy detail beyond the "stars doubled" sentence; section 09's three-paragraph restatement of the Celeste Assist Mode exchange (already told at length in section 05); section 13's "open source does not pay" list (overlaps with section 06's Spolsky paragraph).

Total: ~2,340 words. A second read with the scalpel picks up the rest.

### Target: 8,000 words (~37% cut, "sharp")

Adds ~2,000 further cuts on top of the 10k target.

- **Fold sections 03 and 04 into one Part II opener (~400 words saved).** Kickstarter-as-recognition and devlog-as-cadence are the same argument told through two sets of cases. The merged section runs: Team Cherry, Barone, Bay 12, Caves of Qud, one paragraph on Double Fine documentary practice. Drop the standalone Double Fine opener entirely.
- **Cut section 06's Blender paragraph (~170 words).** Godot carries the open-tooling claim. Blender is corroboration the argument does not need; the "corporate donors named" stretch reads as list. If one sentence survives, it is Ubisoft adopting Blender at the Paris studio.
- **Cut section 07 entirely down to one paragraph (~650 words saved).** The lineage section is backing for a warrant the cases already establish. Keep the Torvalds Usenet post as a one-paragraph lineage note folded into the start of section 06 ("the argument is older than games"). Benkler, Shirky, Doctorow, Buffer, Levels, Indie Hackers go. Cost: the essay loses its "this is older than software" pedigree, which is worth something; but the cases in Parts II and III do the work regardless.
- **Cut section 08 to half (~420 words saved).** The apprenticeship-loss argument is important but carried by one datum (Stanford 13%/20% decline) and one structural claim (OSS is the last training ground). The Copilot/Cursor/Benioff/Zuckerberg stack, the New York Fed / Indeed / Octoverse figures, Fogel, and the Asparouhova caveat can compress to half the current length.
- **Cut section 09's non-games beats (~300 words saved).** Keep Celeste (already told in 05, so this is a reframe), TLOU2, Xbox Adaptive Controller, Forza. Cut Hamilton's business-case numbers (Last Door dyslexia font, MUDRammer, Solara) and the Octoverse / Outreachy graf. The section reduces to: closed pipelines filter, open ones reverse the filter, here are four cases where outside insight shipped.
- **Compress section 10's Valve to ~600 words (~400 words saved).** Keep: leak anecdote, Workshop payouts, Proton/Steam Deck, the closing "open at every layer that touches its players" line. Cut: Newell's LinuxCon quote and piracy quote (they are flavour, not load-bearing), *Free to Play* documentary (PsychOdyssey already carries the AAA-documentary point).

Total additional cut: ~2,340 words. Essay lands near 7,800-8,000.

The cost: the essay becomes narrower. It is still the same argument, but the density of cases that hedge it is lower. Camp A (accelerationist) and Camp C (OSS-economics realist) critiques land a little harder because the evidence layer thins.

### Target: 6,000 words (~53% cut, "minimum viable argument")

Adds ~2,000 further cuts. One case per structural beat. The argument reduced to its skeleton.

- **Part I stays close to full length** (01 and 02, roughly 900 words combined). The setup is load-bearing and already lean; cutting it weakens the hook and the nut graf. Trim section 01's GDC funding paragraph by half; keep the Zukowski beat in 02.
- **Part II compresses to three sections (~2,400 words).**
  - One section on cadence and devlog: Team Cherry (one paragraph), Bay 12 (one), Factorio (one), Celeste Assist Mode exchange (one). Barone, Caves of Qud, Double Fine, Mindustry, Shapez, Blender all fall out. Cost: Shapez is the strongest "open source as funnel" case; its loss weakens section 06's Spolsky beat.
  - One section on open source as a practice: Godot, Torvalds Usenet one-paragraph note, Spolsky. Mindustry and Blender gone.
  - One section on inclusivity / accessibility: Celeste (reuse, not retell), TLOU2, Xbox Adaptive Controller. Forza and the Hamilton numbers gone. The Asparouhova caveat stays; it is the model of the closed-objection move.
- **Cut section 07 entirely (~830 words).** The lineage / theory section. One sentence in Part III opening references Linux.
- **Cut section 08 entirely (~750 words).** The apprenticeship section. Cost: Camp B (Blow / Yu) would say the essay was prescribing visible work without acknowledging the structural shift in how juniors learn; losing this section removes a genuine contribution to the AI debate the essay makes. High cost. Weigh carefully.
- **Part III compresses section 10 to ~400 words.** Valve becomes a one-paragraph case: leak, Workshop payouts, Steam Deck, the "open at every layer that touches its players, closed at the layers that touch its money" line. Cut: the Russell / Anthropic 10% paragraph, the FLI / Bletchley / Seoul / Paris paragraph, the RSP / METR / AISI paragraph. Cost: CRITIQUE-2 Camp A explicitly said the RSP / METR / AISI addition was the highest-leverage fix of round one. Cutting it reopens that seam. - **Section 12 compresses to ~350 words.** Keep the Linux-as-accumulated-trust paragraph and the "closed studio draws only on the people inside the building" paragraph. Cut the Celeste restatement (already told) and the Eleanor Roosevelt quote.
- **Section 13 compresses to ~350 words.** Keep: idea-theft, showing-the-failure, open-source-does-not-pay, the *Airscape* paragraph. Cut: "players do not care" and "risk is too high for a solo". The *Airscape* paragraph is the most important piece of inoculation the essay added in round one (per CRITIQUE-2). Non-negotiable.

Total cut: ~2,000 further words. Essay lands near 6,000.

The cost at 6,000 words is real. The essay becomes closer to a long feature than a mini-book. The Toulmin structure survives; the density of cases does not. Camp A's objection on the AI section and Camp C's on the OSS economics both land harder because the evidence layer is thin. The apprenticeship section's loss removes the essay's most original contribution to the AI displacement debate.

## What every reduction loses

**At 10,000 words.** The Levels.io / Indie Hackers corroboration, some Valve origin density, the theorists paragraph, the Benioff/Zuckerberg flavour, the Roosevelt quote, the summit roll-call. The essay's pedigree thins; the argument does not.

**At 8,000 words.** Section 07 collapses to a sentence. Blender goes. Half of Forza / TLOU2 / Xbox Adaptive Controller stretch goes. Free to Play and Newell's LinuxCon quote go. The reader loses the "older than games" sweep and most of the non-games corroboration.

**At 6,000 words.** The apprenticeship section. Most of the AI safety-initiatives paragraph. The Bay 12 twenty-year arc in full detail. Most of the accessibility cases. PsychOdyssey entirely. The essay reads as a strong long feature rather than a mini-book; the reader gets the argument, not the texture. Camp A and Camp C critiques land harder; the RSP / METR / AISI round-one fix partially reopens.

## Recommended target

**10,000 words (tight).** The essay is a mini-book and was structured as one; at 10,000 words it still reads as one, with the Toulmin architecture intact, every hostile-camp fix from CRITIQUE-2 preserved, and the density of cases that makes the argument hard to dismiss still present. 8,000 would work for a magazine cut where the editor imposes the length; 6,000 reopens two of the seams the critique passes closed and loses the essay's most original contribution (the apprenticeship argument). The canonical version at shuck.dev should be 10,000; an Edge cut at 1,800-2,200 and a magazine cut at 4-7k should be considered separate commissions with their own structural decisions, not outputs of cutting down the canonical draft. Reader completion rates do drop past 6,000 words, but the canonical essay's audience is self-selecting for the long form and the essay earns its length at 10k in a way it does not quite earn at 12,755.
