## Per-claim triangulation proposals

### Claim 1–2: Steam 18,900 games / 80% unplayed (Fifty-One Games a Day)
- Current framing: Global Steam.
- Triangulation opportunity: Steam is the global PC discovery surface, so the global frame is the right one. UKIE confirms the demand side: UK consumer spend on games hit £8.76bn in 2025, +7.4% YoY ([Ukie 2025](https://ukie.org.uk/news/uk-video-games-market-reaches-8-76-billion-growing-7-4-in-2025/)). The dynamic to add is that UK indie supply has grown into the same crowded shelf: UKIE's Annual Review 2025 reports a 24% rise in incorporated games SMEs in Q3 2024 alone ([Ukie Annual Review 2025](https://cms.ukie.org.uk/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/Annual-Review-2025-FINAL.pdf)).
- Proposed addition: a single sentence after the Kotaku line: "The local picture is the same shape. UKIE counted a 24% rise in incorporated UK games SMEs in the third quarter of 2024 alone, into a market where consumers spent £8.76 billion the following year [src:ukie-ar-25][src:ukie-market-25]."
- Cost: ~30 words, one paragraph break to absorb, one new citation pair.
- Recommendation: **Add.** It localises the funnel without diluting the Steam claim.

### Claim 3–4: GDC self-funding and layoffs (Fifty-One Games a Day)
- Current framing: GDC is a global survey but its respondent pool skews US/EU; the essay already names "1 in 3 for US-based developers".
- Triangulation opportunity: The UK has its own number. UKIE reports 3,655 full-time job cuts across 491 UK companies between May 2024 and September 2025, against 2,571 new roles at 513 studios in the same period ([Ukie Annual Review 2025](https://cms.ukie.org.uk/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/Annual-Review-2025-FINAL.pdf)). Ukie has also been campaigning for Government to increase the UK Games Fund, citing a £500m opportunity at risk ([Ukie press release](https://ukie.org.uk/press-releases/ukie-urges-government-to-back-uk-games-industry-or-miss-out-on-500m-opportunity)).
- Proposed addition: append one clause to the layoffs sentence: "In the UK, Ukie's 2025 review counted 3,655 full-time job cuts across 491 companies between May 2024 and September 2025, against 2,571 new roles at 513 studios over the same window [src:ukie-ar-25]."
- Cost: ~35 words. Adds one citation.
- Recommendation: **Add.** It honours the author's home market and shows the squeeze is not only a US story.

### Claim 35: Accessibility ROI (The Last Door, MUDRammer)

## New non-Western cases to add

The Octoverse paragraph names India, Nigeria, Brazil, the Philippines as growth regions but does not put a single developer's name to those flags. Three to add, in priority order. Each is a verifiable case of open or community-anchored development from a non-Western studio that strengthens an existing essay claim.

### Case 1: Polychroma Games (Philippines) — *Until Then*
- Source: Polychroma Games, Manila; *Until Then* released 25 June 2024 on Steam, PS5, Linux. Over 1,000 reviews at 99% positive within weeks of release ([Rappler Q&A](https://www.rappler.com/technology/features/polychroma-games-gameplay-narrative-until-then/), [Game8](https://game8.co/articles/latest/until-then-explores-life-and-culture-in-the-philippines-in-a-narrative-driven-pixel-art-experience), [PhilSTAR Life](https://philstarlife.com/geeky/917153-until-then-philippine-inspired-video-game)).
- What the case proves: A small Filipino team (~10 people, founded 2020 during the pandemic) builds for the audience that has lived the setting; the game's 99% review rating comes substantially from Filipino players seeing themselves in the work, the same recognition mechanism the essay names in *Celeste*'s trans players and TLOU2's accessibility consultants.
- Where it would land: in the Outside the Firm section, after the Octoverse paragraph that names the Philippines, as the named example the paragraph currently lacks. One sentence.
- Word cost: ~30 words.

### Case 2: Toge Productions / Mojiken (Indonesia) — *Coffee Talk*
- Source: Toge Productions, Tangerang; *Coffee Talk* released January 2020. Estimated ~385,000 Steam units, ~US$3M gross revenue ([games-stats.com](https://games-stats.com/steam/game/coffee-talk/)). The studio has published a public devlog series on Medium and itch.io documenting design decisions, music, and mechanics ([Coffee Talk devlog index](https://togeproductions.itch.io/coffee-talk/devlog), [Toge on Medium](https://medium.com/toge-productions/coffee-talk-devlog-2-bringing-doodles-to-life-a3c14c846656)). Coffee Talk began as an internal game jam called Project Green Tea Latte.
- What the case proves: a non-Western studio practising the exact Friday-cadence devlog discipline the essay walks Wube and Bay 12 through; the open practice scales geographically.
- Where it would land: in the Friday After Friday section, as a one-sentence coda after the Caves of Qud paragraph. The payoff is that the cadence is not a Western-studio habit.
- Word cost: ~35 words.

### Case 3: Rasheed Abueideh (Palestine) — *Liyla and the Shadows of War*
- Source: Solo developer in Nablus, West Bank; released 2016 free on iOS, Android, Windows. The Apple App Store initially refused to list it as a "game" before reversing position ([Game Developer coverage](https://www.gamedeveloper.com/business/apple-classifies-palestinian-developed-game-as-not-a-game-due-to-war-theme), [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Liyla_and_the_Shadows_of_War), [Gamesauce profile](https://www.gamesauce.biz/2016/06/11/rasheed-abu-eideh-bringing-the-shadows-of-war-to-light/)). Won Excellence in Storytelling at the 2016 IMGA MENA.
- What the case proves: the "the load-bearing insight came from someone who had lived it" claim, in its sharpest form. A game about civilians under bombardment, made by a developer living through the same war, distributed openly because no commercial publisher would have shipped it.
- Where it would land: in the Outside the Firm section, between the AAA accessibility cases and the Octoverse paragraph, as the case that proves the principle scales from disability to lived political reality.
- Word cost: ~45 words.

## UK-specific context worth adding

The author lives in the UK, the essay is currently silent on UK conditions, and at least one UK beat is now warranted without parochialism.

1. **UKIE 2025 jobs and SME numbers.** Already covered above (Claims 1–4): a single sentence in Fifty-One Games a Day adds the UK shape of the squeeze. ~30–35 words. **High priority.**
2. **UK Games Fund.** The DCMS-backed UKGF has supported 100+ UK indie studios with prototype grants up to £25,000 since 2015 ([UK Games Fund](https://ukgamesfund.com/), [Gov.uk evaluation](https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/evaluation-of-the-uk-games-fund/evaluation-of-the-uk-games-fund)). It is a public funding mechanism that complements rather than replaces the open-development practice the essay argues for. **Worth one sentence in the Fears in Order section, beside "Open source does not pay"**, as evidence that hybrid public-grant + open-development funding paths exist. ~25 words. **Medium priority.**
3. **Equality Act 2010.** Covered above (Claim 35). **Lower priority.**

The essay should not become a UK essay; the goal is one or two UK beats that read as the author writing from somewhere, not as advocacy for UK policy.

## US framing honesty

The essay borrows several global-shaped statistics that are in fact US-skewed. Where to name this honestly:

- **The Rung That Disappeared (highest priority).** Stanford ADP payroll, NY Fed labor market tracker, Indeed Hiring Lab, SignalFire State of Talent Report: every dataset in the section is US-only. The fix is one phrase: open the data paragraph with "In the US labour market, where the data is sharpest…". This concedes nothing; it makes the section honest. **Recommend: apply.**
- **GDC State of the Industry.** Already partially honest (the essay names "1 in 3 for US-based developers"). The 82% self-funding figure is also weighted toward Anglosphere respondents. The current phrasing is acceptable; UK addition above tightens it.
- **TLOU2 / Xbox Adaptive Controller / Forza Horizon 5.** These are explicitly US studios making US/global products; no honesty issue.
- **Steam-wide statistics (Claims 1–2, 6, 41–42).** These are global by construction. Steam is the global discovery surface; no fix required.
- **GitHub Octoverse.** Already global, with non-Western beats. No fix.
