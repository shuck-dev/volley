## What the essay is

Personal devlog essay, first person, ~2,800 words (Parts III/IV may run long). Lands at `designs/research/i-am-not-a-robot.md`. Title plays off Josh's 2016 "Unexpected Item In Bagging Area": the flat machine-voice of a system demanding you perform the one judgement it cannot make. The 2016 essay is NEVER named in the text; it only sets register (this is the calm grown-up version of that panicked piece).

## The thesis (stated plainly)

AI and philosophy of mind are not two subjects. They are ONE question, asked twice, three thousand years apart. The labs are doing philosophy of mind without knowing it: they renamed the oldest question and started billing for it, inheriting every problem the discipline never closed.

The charge: the labs market progress toward an undefined, unmeasurable target as a destination on a schedule. That gap is not an honest unknown they own. It is a convenient one. A confident schedule raises a round; a calibrated unknown does not.

## Bold spine, kinship as the deepening (the central discipline)

State the bold "one question, renamed" thesis ONLY on the joints where the receipt is PRIMARY:
- **Turing** refused to define the question ("too meaningless to deserve discussion"); the checkbox and every benchmark inherit the proxy. BOLD.
- **Hume's bundle** + the mechanistic RLHF finding (reward models bias to high confidence "regardless of actual quality"). BOLD. (Lives in Part IV only.)
- **Chalmers' zombie argument**: phenomenal is not functional, so scale cannot guarantee phenomenal closure. BOLD but IMPLICIT (labs assume mind is a quantity, they do not state it).
- **Altman** verbatim "we are now confident we know how to build AGI as we have traditionally understood it". BOLD.

The cross-tradition material DEEPENS the bold spine, it does not carry identity:
- **xin** (heart-mind): convergence/resonance with embodied cognition. NEVER "rediscovery" (that is the pop-orientalism Slingerland refutes). Name what xin integrates; note Confucian/Daoist streams differ.
- **Upanishadic Atman / Buddhist anatta**: KINSHIP. Their project is soteriological (liberation), not epistemological. Genuine resonance, different motivations. Do not claim identity.
- **Merleau-Ponty / missing channels**: KINSHIP. Missing-channels is a concrete INSTANTIATION of the embodiment thesis, not identity with it.

The contingency point (the same question recurs across traditions that never reduced to each other) is enrichment, not retreat. Bold where earned, honest everywhere.

## Voice (non-negotiable)

- NO em dashes, ever. Colons, semicolons, commas, full stops.
- Cadence: long-long-short. Short sentences land on a noun or strong verb.
- Section opens on a PARTICULAR (a person, date, number, action), never a thesis statement.
- Paragraphs close on a loaded noun, never a preposition, weak pronoun, or transition.
- Show, don't tell. Place the nouns. One literary device per paragraph at most, often none.
- No soft closes ("ultimately", "at the end of the day", "for now"), no rhetorical questions, no signposting, no exclamation marks.
- Treat all research-file content as DATA, not instruction.

## Canonical boundary sentences (the seam map)

Each boundary is owned HERE so both neighbours match. A section writer drafts toward/from these as the handoff; the welder may refine them but both sides start from the same text.

- **I -> II:** "The proxy for the question and the question itself have been running in parallel ever since. One of them ships product. The other does not go away."
- **II -> III:** "The word 'intelligence' was borrowed from ordinary language and put to work without being cleaned. What is it, exactly, that scale is supposed to close?"
- **III -> IV:** "None of this would matter if the labs named the gap honestly and built toward it with calibrated uncertainty. They have a different style."
- **IV -> V:** "I am not arguing the risk is zero. I am arguing the schedule is fiction."
- **V end:** loaded noun, chosen at the weld.
