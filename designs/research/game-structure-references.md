# Game Structure References

Volley is a three-register game (Construction, Reality, Reconstruction) and the team needs lineage for what other games have done with comparable shapes. This doc collects case studies the design spike (SH-278) and the artist world bible can lean on. The recommendations sit at the end and the cases earn them.

## The two-world tradition

Omori is the closest existing parallel. Omocat shipped it in December 2020 after six and a half years of development at the studio of the same name, an indie that began as a Tumblr webcomic the artist used to cope with a confusing stretch of life. The game splits between Headspace, an imaginary world where the protagonist plays as Omori with three friends in vivid pastel, and Faraway Town, the real world where the protagonist is Sunny, a hikikomori who has not left his room in years. Headspace was conceived from lucid dreaming and runs on the logic of a child's dream. Faraway Town runs on the logic of unhealed trauma. The cracks bleed. The decision to enter the real world is the player's, and the game's hardest moments are not the boss fights in Headspace but the trips downstairs.

What works for Omori is the asymmetry of fullness. Headspace is bright and busy and has friends in it; Faraway Town is empty rooms and small errands. The player feels which one is preferable before the game ever explains why. The construction is not a trick; it is a coping mechanism the player participates in. When the construction fails, the player has helped maintain it long enough to feel the cost of letting it go. ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Omori_(video_game)), [academic study](https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/15554120231162982))

For Volley, the asymmetry-of-fullness lesson transfers. The fullness gap between Construction and Reality is not a graphical downgrade; it is an emotional one. What does not transfer is Omori's binary toggle. Volley wants three registers, not two, and the move from Reality to Reconstruction is the move Omori does not make.

Persona 4 (Atlus, 2008) and Persona 5 (Atlus, 2016) sit one shelf over. Both run a daytime register, school and town and friendships, against a cognition register: the Midnight Channel TV World in Persona 4, the Metaverse and its Palaces in Persona 5. The cognition register is built from a person's distortion. Kamoshida's palace in Persona 5 is his school rendered as his castle, with the female students he preys on appearing as statues. The Treasure at the centre of the Palace is the symbol his desire fixed on; for Kamoshida, it is a volleyball trophy. The Persona series makes the construction someone else's, then drops the player inside it. ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Persona_5), [Megami Tensei Wiki on Palaces](https://megamitensei.fandom.com/wiki/Palace))

What works for Persona is the explicit architecture. The cognition register has rules, a Treasure, and a deadline. The player learns to read someone's distortion the way an architect reads a building. What may not transfer to Volley is the externalisation. Persona puts the broken interior in someone else; Volley's broken interior is the protagonist's, and the construction is theirs to maintain.

Catherine (Atlus, 2011) holds the failure-prone shape. The day is social simulation at the Stray Sheep bar, where Vincent talks himself in circles. The night is a vertical tower of falling blocks Vincent climbs as a sheep. The construction is the daytime's deniability; the nightmares are the price. ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catherine_(video_game)))

Silent Hill 2 (Team Silent, Konami, 2001) is the case where the construction is the horror. James enters the foggy town to find a wife who has been dead long enough that the search itself is the symptom. The Otherworld pulses underneath the Fog World and gets louder; Pyramid Head is James's guilt with a body. The cracks are deniable for a long time. The reveal does not arrive as a twist; it arrives as the inability to keep denying. The story was conceived by Takayoshi Sato from Crime and Punishment, and the descent is the structural figure: down the trail, down the stairs, down the elevator, down. ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Silent_Hill_2), [Otherworld design analysis](https://horror.dreamdawn.com/?p=29211))

The Beginner's Guide (Davey Wreden, Everything Unlimited, 2015) inverts the relationship: the construction is the protagonist, and the player's job is to interpret it. Wreden narrates a tour through the games of a developer named Coda, drawing conclusions about Coda's mental state from the levels. The game is about the way Wreden's reading reshapes what the player sees, and about the harm that reading has done. The two worlds are the games and the narration; the crack is the player's growing suspicion that the narrator is not to be trusted. ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Beginner%27s_Guide), [Steam](https://store.steampowered.com/app/303210/The_Beginners_Guide/))

For Volley, The Beginner's Guide is the warning. A construction maintained by a narrator can become a violation when the narrator uses it for their own ends. Volley's construction belongs to the protagonist and is maintained by them, not interpreted from the outside; the lesson is that the relationship between maintainer and construction has to be clean.

Spec Ops: The Line (Yager, 2K, 2012) takes the descent shape and makes it cumulative. Captain Walker enters Dubai and the player follows. The white phosphorus scene is mandatory: the player aims a mortar through a thermal display, sees something they think is enemies, fires, and walks through the aftermath to find civilians. The break is both gradual and singular. The cracks have been visible for hours, in loading screens, in posters, in the way the soldier the player controls speaks to his squad. The phosphorus is the moment the deniability breaks. ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spec_Ops:_The_Line), [Mark Brown's GMTK retrospective](https://gmtk.substack.com/p/why-spec-ops-the-line-mattered))

The both-cumulative-and-singular shape is exactly the shape Volley's break wants. Cracks pile up; one moment is the moment the maintenance fails. Spec Ops also models the consent the player has been giving to the construction by playing along, which is a different relationship than horror's involuntary descent.

## Multi-register beyond two

Outer Wilds (Mobius Digital, Annapurna, 2019) stacks registers rather than alternating them. The player explores a planetary system locked in a 22-minute loop that ends when the sun goes supernova. The loop is one register; the supernova is another; the quantum moon is a third; the Eye of the universe is a fourth. None of them replaces the others. Knowledge persists where progress does not; the player ends with the same ship and the same jetpack and a mind reshaped by what they have learned. ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Outer_Wilds), [Steam](https://store.steampowered.com/app/753640/Outer_Wilds/))

For Volley, the lesson is that registers can layer rather than alternate. The Reconstruction is not Construction with the cracks repaired; it is Construction after Reality, and what changes is what the player carries into it. Outer Wilds shows how to make the carrying load-bearing: knowledge, not stat lines, is the bridge between registers.

Slay the Princess (Black Tabby Games, 2023) reshapes the construction with each visit. Tony Howard-Arias and Abby Howard built a horror visual novel in which the Princess in the cabin is whatever the player believed her to be the last time. Each loop alters the next. The game's trick is that the player's perception is the world's metaphysics; what you believe she is, she becomes. Chapter 2 reveals the loop, and the construction reveals itself as a perception problem rather than a level. ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Slay_the_Princess), [Game Developer deep dive](https://www.gamedeveloper.com/design/deep-dive-player-centered-narrative-design-in-slay-the-princess))

13 Sentinels: Aegis Rim (Vanillaware, Atlus, 2019) hands the player the parts of the story and lets them assemble it. Thirteen high-school protagonists across five eras (1945, 1985, 2025, 2065, 2105) live their adventure segments in any order. The player unlocks chapters by satisfying conditions that span characters; the structure is a graph rather than a path. The reveal is something the player puts together rather than something the game shows them. ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/13_Sentinels:_Aegis_Rim))

For Volley, 13 Sentinels is a counterexample. The lesson is cautionary: when the structure fragments enough, the emotional through-line goes with it. Volley's through-line is the protagonist's, and the registers serve that.

## The deniable-crack pattern

Doki Doki Literature Club (Team Salvato, Dan Salvato, 2017) hides the horror under the dating sim. The opening hours are ordinary visual-novel beats; the cracks start small (a misplaced sprite, a dialogue line that does not fit) and end with Monika deleting other characters' files from the player's disk. The construction is the genre, and the deniability is the player's willingness to read those small cracks as polish errors rather than warnings. ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Doki_Doki_Literature_Club!))

The Stanley Parable (Davey Wreden and William Pugh, Galactic Cafe remake 2013) puts the crack in the narrator. Kevan Brighting voices an omnipresent guide; Stanley walks through an office complex; whether Stanley follows the narration or contradicts it shapes the next room. The construction is the office. The crack is the disagreement between the narrator's plan and Stanley's feet. ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Stanley_Parable))

Pony Island (Daniel Mullins, 2016) puts the crack in the interface. The player loads a pony-jumping arcade game and finds it corrupted; the puzzles are about hacking around Lucifer, who controls the cabinet. The Steam API gets used to fake friend notifications. The chrome around the game is the construction, and the cracks are interface failures the player has to repair. ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pony_Island))

Inscryption (Daniel Mullins, 2021) stacks four layers. The cabin where a shadowy figure deals cards is one layer; a retro 2D pixel game is the second; a return to 3D with broken rules is the third; found-footage videos thread under all of them as a fourth. The cracks are not anomalies; the construction's instability is the rule of the game. ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Inscryption))

Across this pattern, the lesson for Volley is consistent. A crack is dismissible by the player only if the construction has earned the player's investment first. Doki Doki spends an hour as a dating sim before the first sprite glitch. Stanley walks through several offices before the narrator's voice strains. Pony Island looks like a corrupt arcade for some time before the Steam notifications start. Volley's cracks need the same patience. The construction has to be enjoyable on its own terms first; the cracks earn their dismissibility from the construction's prior generosity.

## Construction-and-maintenance

Disco Elysium (ZA/UM, 2019) makes the construction the protagonist's self. Robert Kurvitz and team partitioned Harry Du Bois into 24 internal voices grouped under four attributes (Intellect, Psyche, Physique, Motorics). Logic argues with Empathy; Inland Empire whispers what objects think. The Thought Cabinet holds beliefs the player has decided to internalise; each takes time to research and changes Harry while it does. The construction is not a place; it is a person, kept together by the voices the player has invested in. ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Disco_Elysium), [Reverse Shot on the skill system](https://reverseshot.org/features/2788/righteous_skills))

For Volley, Disco Elysium points at the protagonist's interiority as a register in its own right. The call-of-the-void layer in the MC profile is exactly the kind of internal weight that wants a home in the design without ever being narrated. Disco Elysium's lesson is that interiority can be made playable without exposition.

Stardew Valley (Eric Barone, ConcernedApe, 2016) opens with a grandfather's letter and a cubicle: the protagonist quits JojaMart and inherits the farm. The construction is the farm. The maintenance is the daily loop of crops, livestock, friendship, and the slow restoration of the Community Center against Joja's offer to bulldoze it. Barone has since said the Community-versus-Joja framing was too black and white; the version that shipped is a construction whose effort is its meaning. ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stardew_Valley), [Eric Barone interview, GamesRadar](https://www.gamesradar.com/games/simulation/if-i-made-stardew-valley-again-i-would-try-to-be-a-little-more-nuanced-eric-barone-says-the-community-center-versus-jojamart-battle-was-too-black-and-white/))

Spiritfarer (Thunder Lotus, 2020) lays cozy management on top of death stewardship. Stella is a ferrymaster; the player builds a boat, cooks for spirit passengers, hugs them, and takes each to the door at the end. The construction is the boat and the gardens and the cooking, and the maintenance is the daily care that lets the player face the goodbye when it comes. ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spiritfarer), [Thunder Lotus design notes](https://www.gamedeveloper.com/design/inside-the-thoughtful-design-of-thunder-lotus-i-spiritfarer-i-))

Lake (Gamious, 2021) sets Meredith Weiss in 1986 Providence Oaks, Oregon, where she covers her father's mail route for two weeks. There is no break, no horror, no escalation. The construction is the small-town pretense and Meredith's choice of whether to stay; the cosiness is the medium for the question. ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lake_(video_game)), [Steam](https://store.steampowered.com/app/1118240/Lake/))

Animal Crossing: New Horizons (Nintendo, March 2020) is the construction that never breaks. It shipped the day after the first US stay-at-home order; the game became the register many millions of players spent the early pandemic inside. The construction holds because the maintenance is the game. ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Animal_Crossing:_New_Horizons))

The construction-and-maintenance shelf clarifies a thing Volley already half-knows. The daily loop is not scaffolding around the meaning; the loop is where the meaning lives. Stardew's effort, Spiritfarer's care, Lake's small choices, Animal Crossing's perfect deniability: each is a model for the warmth that has to hold in Volley's Construction before any crack lands.

## What this teaches Volley

Five recommendations the spike can pick up.

**Build Construction's fullness as the load-bearing register.** Omori, Stardew, Spiritfarer, Animal Crossing all earn their meaning by making the constructed register a place the player wants to be in for its own sake. The asymmetry between Construction and Reality is not a downgrade; it is what gives the eventual lift to Reconstruction its weight.

**Cracks earn their dismissibility from the construction's generosity.** Doki Doki, Pony Island, Stanley Parable, Silent Hill 2 all front-load construction time before the first crack lands. Volley's cracks should arrive only after the player has invested enough that the dismissal is theirs to make.

**The break is both cumulative and singular.** Spec Ops names the shape Volley's bible already gestures at. Pile the cracks; let one moment be the moment the maintenance fails. The cumulative half makes the singular moment land; the singular moment makes the cumulative half retrospectively coherent.

**Make Reconstruction the carrying register, not the repaired one.** Outer Wilds is the model: knowledge changes the player without changing the system. Reconstruction is Construction after Reality, and what differs is what the protagonist now holds. Treat Reconstruction as a tonal lift over the same register, not a third asset set conceived from scratch.

**Hold the call of the void as undertow, not theme.** Disco Elysium and the protagonist profile both point at this: the load-bearing interior layer is one the player feels in posture, hesitation, and what the camera does not look at. Disco Elysium's voices are an existence proof that interiority is playable without exposition. Volley should never narrate the void; it should let the picture imply it.

## Open questions for further research

- Reconstruction precedent. Outer Wilds is the closest match for the carrying-knowledge shape, but a closer look at games that explicitly mature their constructed register, rather than swap it, would help. NieR: Automata's Route C, Undertale's True Pacifist run, and Hades's narrative loop are candidates worth a second pass.
- The mini-game-inside-Reality question. Volley wants volleyball to appear in Reality as an optional mini-game with a different tonal weight; precedents for activities that survive the register break (Yakuza's arcades, Witcher 3's Gwent) deserve a pass focused on tonal continuity rather than mechanical similarity.
- Length and pacing. Most of the cases here are 8-to-30-hour single-player games; Volley is an idle game with a different attention contract. Register transitions in idle and ambient games (Cookie Clicker's Grandmapocalypse, Universal Paperclips' phase shifts) deserve their own short study.
