# Angle 6: Market Context

Research notes for the stage-setting paragraph. Grounded facts only.

## 1. Steam release volume

- 2024: ~18,900 games released on Steam, a record. Up ~32% from 2023. Source: SteamDB stats aggregated by multiple outlets.
  - https://steamdb.info/stats/releases/
  - https://www.pcgamer.com/games/nearly-19-000-games-released-on-steam-in-2024/
- 2023: ~14,000 games released. Source: SteamDB via TweakTown.
  - https://www.tweaktown.com/news/102333/steam-saw-close-to-19-000-pc-games-released-throughout-2024-32-more-than-2023/index.html
- Felt scale: ~51 new games per day in 2024, roughly 360 per week.
- Visibility floor: Kotaku analysis of 2024 releases reported ~80% had minimal player counts.
  - https://kotaku.com/steam-19-000-new-games-limited-pc-valve-unplayed-80-1851738322

## 2. Discoverability (Chris Zukowski, How To Market A Game)

- Rule of thumb for Popular Upcoming: "Minimum is 7000 [wishlists], or thereabouts." Threshold floats with the week's competition; some games surface at 5,500 in quiet weeks.
- On the algorithm: "the Steam algorithm is 'tuned' and has a set expectation of what success looks like. And if you reach those thresholds of success, Steam shows more of your game, and if sells less than your game is shown less."
- Framing quote he opens with (Jim Butcher): "You don't have to run faster than the bear to get away. You just have to run faster than the guy next to you." Captures the relative nature of Steam visibility.
- Source: https://howtomarketagame.com/2022/09/26/how-many-wishlists-should-i-have-when-i-launch-my-game/
- Related (algorithm myths): https://howtomarketagame.com/2023/09/04/killing-the-myths-behind-steams-visibility/

## 3. Layoff wave (2023-2025)

- 2023: ~10,500 recorded layoffs (Farhan Noor's tracker).
- 2024: ~14,600 recorded layoffs. Peak month was January 2024.
- 2025: ~4,000+ logged through mid-year; pace easing but not halted.
- Cumulative 2022 through mid-2025: ~45,000 jobs lost.
- Headline closures and cuts: Microsoft's 2024 closure of Arkane Austin, Tango Gameworks, and Roundhouse Studios; Embracer Group's multi-studio wind-downs (including Volition); cuts at Unity, EA, Sony Interactive, Take-Two, Ubisoft, Sega, Riot.
- Sources:

  - https://www.pcgamer.com/the-impact-of-16000-games-industry-layoffs-in-one-chart/
  - https://www.gamedeveloper.com/business/industry-layoffs-are-seemingly-slowing-but-the-damage-has-already-been-done

## 4. Marketing ceiling / cost-per-wishlist

- No single canonical cost-per-wishlist figure publicly holds up; Zukowski and GameDiscoverCo both note paid-ad wishlists convert worse than organic.
- Developer of Furnish Master reported "wishlists from ads were of worse quality [and] converted into sales less effectively" than organic.
  - https://newsletter.gamediscover.co/p/the-state-of-steam-wishlist-conversions
- Wishlist-to-sales reality (GameDiscoverCo): median 0.15x conversion in Week 1 for games with >25k wishlists at launch; drops to 0.10x for games priced above $10. Individual games vary by 10-20x around the median.
- Implication: a marginal paid wishlist at any plausible ad CPA (commonly cited anecdotally in the $1-3 range) converts to roughly $0.10-$0.30 of Week-1 revenue at a $10+ price point. Buying visibility rarely pencils out for a small indie.

## 5. GDC State of the Industry (2024 and 2025)

- 2025 survey: share of developers working 40 hours or less per week fell from 64% to 57%. Share working 51+ hours rose from 8% to 13%. First increase in overwork since the question was introduced in 2019.
- Overtime motivation: 67% "own initiative," 14% fear of consequences, 12% direct management pressure.
- Pay gaps (2025): people of colour earn 27% less than white colleagues; women earn 24% less than men.
- Funding reality: 82% of indie developers have put their own money into their games; roughly half of all developers are self-funding.
- Layoff exposure: 1 in 10 developers laid off in the past year; 1 in 4 in the past two years, with nearly half of those still job-hunting.
