# Angle 2: Public Development as Work

Notes on three projects where public development was part of how the game got made. Primary sources preferred.

## Stardew Valley (Eric Barone / ConcernedApe)

- Dev began 2011/2012; Greenlight announce September 2012; 1.0 shipped 26 Feb 2016. ~4 years of visible dev before launch. [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stardew_Valley).
- Public channels: blog at `stardewvalley.blogspot.com` (2012-13) then `stardewvalley.net/blog` (2013+); Chucklefish forum subboard (`community.playstarbound.com/forums/stardew-valley.72/`); Twitter, Reddit, Steam Greenlight. [Dev Blog index](https://stardewvalleywiki.com/Developer_Blog).
- Chucklefish signed on as publisher mid-dev, following the public presence, not preceding it.
- Concrete feedback-driven change: Barone redrew every villager portrait after forum users said the bachelorettes looked too similar / unappealing; before-and-after comparisons were posted publicly during the redesign. [Game Developer retrospective](https://www.gamedeveloper.com/business/the-4-years-of-self-imposed-crunch-that-went-into-i-stardew-valley-i-).
- Barone, direct quote: "My strategy with the community is simple: no strategy at all. I think that, as an indie developer, you should just be yourself and be a real human." [Seattle Indies](https://seattleindies.org/from-farm-to-fame-eric-barone-concernedape-on-stardew-valleys-journey-and-the-evolving-indie-scene/).
- Sales: 41M+ lifetime by end of 2024 (26M PC, 7.9M Switch); ~$518M gross. [Game Developer, 2025](https://www.gamedeveloper.com/business/stardew-valley-sales-grew-to-over-41-million-by-2024-s-end).

## Dwarf Fortress (Bay 12 Games, Tarn & Zach Adams)

- DF dev began October 2002; first public alpha August 2006; free release on bay12games.com ran 2006-2022; paid Steam/itch release 6 Dec 2022. [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dwarf_Fortress).
- Monthly devlogs on bay12games.com since the early 2000s; each one is a financial state-of-studio plus next-month plan. Cadence uninterrupted for 20+ years. [Bay 12](https://www.bay12games.com/dwarves/), [DF Wiki: Dev logs](https://dwarffortresswiki.org/index.php/Dev_logs).
- bay12forums.com Suggestions subforum has fed features into releases for two decades. [DF Wiki: Community](https://dwarffortresswiki.org/index.php/Community_of_Dwarf_Fortress).
- Patreon launched early 2015; moving from PayPal donations to Patreon "doubled" monthly revenue per Tarn. Patrons were the sole income until Steam release. Full-time dev since 2007. [MCV](https://mcvuk.com/business-news/publishing/dwarf-fortress-dev-moving-from-donations-to-patreon-doubled-our-income/), [Patreon blog](https://blog.patreon.com/creator-of-the-week-bay12games).
- Tarn, direct quotes: fans "are the reason I've been able to make the step from hobbyist to full-time developer"; on player narratives, "it's really gratifying, because it's one of the things we set out to do." [PC Gamer](https://www.pcgamer.com/community-heroes-tarn-adams-for-dwarf-fortress/).
- Steam sales: 160K in 24h (Dec 2022), 300K in 6 days, ~500K first month, 800K+ by Apr 2024, 1M+ since. [Game Developer](https://www.gamedeveloper.com/business/-i-dwarf-fortress-i-hits-2-months-sales-target-in-24-hours), [GWO 2024](https://gameworldobserver.com/2024/04/04/dwarf-fortress-sales-800k-steam-1-million-wishlists).

## Caves of Qud (Freehold Games, Brian Bucklew & Jason Grinblat)

- Dev began 2007; first public beta 2010; Steam Early Access 15 July 2015; 1.0 on 5 Dec 2024. ~14 years in public beta/EA. [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Caves_of_Qud), [Games Press](https://www.gamespress.com/CAVES-OF-QUD-10-LAUNCHES-DECEMBER-5-AFTER-15-YEARS-OF-DEVELOPMENT).
- Patreon launched 3 May 2019. Tiers $1-$99; perks include pet/tile voting, credits, beta access. [Launch tweet](https://x.com/freeholdgames/status/1124398887777660928), [Wiki](https://wiki.cavesofqud.com/wiki/Freehold_Games_Patreon).
- Devblog cadence: weekly "Feature Friday" patch + post on itch.io and Steam, paused only around Big Beta runs. [Itch devlog](https://freeholdgames.itch.io/cavesofqud/devlog).
- On durability: the team stayed in Early Access nine years because they "wanted a positive lifestyle and great game, rather than maximum profit." [GamesRadar](https://www.gamesradar.com/games/roguelike/legendary-roguelike-rpgs-9-years-in-steam-early-access-was-possible-as-devs-wanted-a-positive-lifestyle-and-great-game-rather-than-maximum-profit/).
- Feedback is structurally wired in: patrons vote on pets and tiles that ship in live patches; beta branches go to patrons first. [Wiki](https://wiki.cavesofqud.com/wiki/Freehold_Games_Patreon).
- Sales: smaller scale; no public lifetime figure, but 1.0 performance funded a Switch port. [PC Gamer via Yahoo](https://tech.yahoo.com/gaming/articles/im-not-sure-rational-decision-214002277.html).

## Cross-cutting observations

- All three treat feedback as dev input, not promo output: Barone redrew art after forum critique; Bay 12 runs a 20-year Suggestions forum; Freehold ships patron-voted content.
- All three pre-funded full-time dev through public channels before a commercial launch: Barone via publisher deal that followed public traction; Bay 12 via donations then Patreon (2015); Freehold via Early Access (2015) then Patreon (2019).
- Cadence is long and boring by design: monthly (Bay 12), weekly (Freehold), irregular but frequent (Barone). Durability beats any single post.
