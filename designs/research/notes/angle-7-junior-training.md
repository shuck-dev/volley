# Angle 7: Junior Training Pathways, AI, and Open Source as Apprenticeship

Reportorial notes. Quotes verbatim where marked. Flag where a figure is widely repeated but traces to a single source.

## 1. Entry-level tech hiring collapse, 2023–2025

- **Stanford / Brynjolfsson, Chandar, Chen, "Canaries in the Coal Mine?" (Aug 2025).** First-of-its-kind payroll-data study (ADP records through July 2025). Key finding: **13% relative decline in employment for early-career workers (22–25) in the most AI-exposed jobs** since widespread generative-AI adoption. Software developers 22–25: **employment down ~20% from late-2022 peak by September 2025.** Workers 30+ in the same AI-exposed categories grew 6–12% over the same window. (src: https://digitaleconomy.stanford.edu/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/Canaries_BrynjolfssonChandarChen.pdf ; coverage: https://www.cnbc.com/2025/08/28/generative-ai-reshapes-us-job-market-stanford-study-shows-entry-level-young-workers.html ; https://fortune.com/2025/08/26/stanford-ai-entry-level-jobs-gen-z-erik-brynjolfsson/ ; https://time.com/7312205/ai-jobs-stanford/)

- **NY Fed Labor Market for Recent College Graduates (2025).** Recent-grad unemployment ~5.7% Q4 2025; underemployment 42.5% (highest since 2020). **Computer science recent-grad unemployment 6.1%; computer engineering 7.5%** — higher than art history (3%), English (4.9%), performing arts (2.7%). (src: https://www.newyorkfed.org/research/college-labor-market ; summary: https://www.newsweek.com/computer-science-popular-college-major-has-one-highest-unemployment-rates-2076514)

- **Indeed software-developer postings: -71% Feb 2022 → Aug 2025.** Widely cited; originates from Indeed Hiring Lab data. (via https://www.cnn.com/2025/08/28/tech/computer-science-graduates-job-hunt-ai)

- **Big-tech entry-level:** new-grad hires fell **~25% from 2023 to 2024** at the 15 largest tech firms; new grads are now ~7% of new hires at Big Tech, down >50% vs. 2019. Source: SignalFire State of Talent Report, repeatedly cited. (coverage: https://sfstandard.com/2025/05/20/silicon-valley-white-collar-recession-entry-level/ ; https://newsletter.pragmaticengineer.com/p/state-of-the-tech-market-in-2025)

- **Caveat:** the "programmer employment -27.5% 2023–2025" figure circulates widely but BLS "computer programmer" is a shrinking legacy SOC code distinct from "software developer"; treat as directional, not clean. (https://spectrum.ieee.org/ai-effect-entry-level-jobs)

## 2. AI coding tools as the proximate cause

- **GitHub Copilot: 20M all-time users by July 2025** (TechCrunch, from GitHub's own Q4 FY25 disclosure); used at 90% of Fortune 100. (src: https://techcrunch.com/2025/07/30/github-copilot-crosses-20-million-all-time-users/)
- **Cursor / Anysphere:** >1M paying customers, $500M ARR by June 2025, $2B ARR by early 2026; used at >50% of Fortune 500. (src: https://techcrunch.com/2025/06/05/cursors-anysphere-nabs-9-9b-valuation-soars-past-500m-arr/ ; https://thenextweb.com/news/cursor-anysphere-2-billion-funding-50-billion-valuation-ai-coding)

Paraphrase from the same remarks: "in engineering this year at Salesforce, we're seriously debating — maybe we aren't going to hire anybody this year." (src: https://www.salesforceben.com/salesforce-will-hire-no-more-software-engineers-in-2025-says-marc-benioff/ ; https://sfstandard.com/2025/02/27/salesforce-marcbenioff-layoffs-tech-agents/)

- **Mark Zuckerberg, Joe Rogan Experience, Jan 10 2025 (verbatim):** "Probably in 2025 we at Meta, as well as the other companies that are basically working on this, are going to have an AI that can effectively be a sort of midlevel engineer at your company that can write code." (src coverage: https://www.entrepreneur.com/business-news/meta-developing-ai-engineers-to-write-code-instead-of-humans/485806 ; https://www.itpro.com/software/development/a-sign-of-things-to-come-in-software-development-mark-zuckerberg-says-ai-will-be-doing-the-work-of-mid-level-engineers-this-year-and-hes-not-the-only-big-tech-exec-predicting-the-end-of-the-profession)

## 3. Open source as an apprenticeship (still intact)

- **GitHub Octoverse 2024:** 1.4M new developers joined open source globally in 2024; generative-AI project contributions +59% YoY, projects +98%; no rise in rejected PR rate, i.e., new contributors were absorbed, not rebuffed. (src: https://github.blog/news-insights/octoverse/octoverse-2024/)
- **Octoverse 2023:** "the state of open source and rise of AI" framed OSS as primary on-ramp for AI-era skills. (src: https://github.blog/news-insights/research/the-state-of-open-source-and-ai/)

- **Nadia Asparouhova, *Working in Public* (2020).** Reframes modern OSS as a creator/maintainer economy rather than guild collaboration; relevant caution for the apprenticeship argument — she notes maintainers' problem is often *too many* low-quality drive-by contributions, not too few contributors. Apprenticeship happens in the sustained-contributor tail, not the first PR. (src: https://nadia.xyz/open-source/ ; https://nayafia.substack.com/p/22-working-in-public)

- **Karl Fogel, *Producing Open Source Software* (producingoss.com).** Canonical on the social mechanics juniors pick up in public projects: code review norms, commit-message discipline, RFC-style disagreement, release management, scope negotiation. Free, still maintained.

- **GSoC, Outreachy, Hacktoberfest, Google Season of Docs** — formal mentored on-ramps; GSoC alone has 700+ participating orgs annually. (src: https://navendu.me/posts/open-source-internship-programs/)

## 4. The apprenticeship-gap argument

- **Addy Osmani, "AI Won't Kill Junior Devs — But Your Hiring Strategy Might" (2025):** articulates the "missing rung" — if juniors aren't hired, there's no pipeline to mid/senior in 3–5 years. (src: https://addyo.substack.com/p/ai-wont-kill-junior-devs-but-your)
- **HN discussion "The ladder is missing rungs" (2025):** practitioner consensus that AI ate the middle and kicked out the bottom rung. (src: https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47574346)
- **GitHub, "Rethinking open source mentorship in the AI era" (2025):** GitHub itself arguing OSS maintainership is where code review, public accountability, and release discipline still get taught. (src: https://github.blog/open-source/maintainers/rethinking-open-source-mentorship-in-the-ai-era/)
- **Stack Overflow, "AI vs Gen Z" (Dec 2025):** traces the changed career path for juniors and points to community contribution as remaining skill-forming venue. (src: https://stackoverflow.blog/2025/12/26/ai-vs-gen-z/)

**Connecting thread for the essay:** juniors historically absorbed code review, scope negotiation, release cadence, and handling disagreement on the job. AI coding tools absorbed the task-level work that used to justify that hire; public-facing open development (OSS, public roadmaps, public playtests) remains one of the few environments where those specific skills are still practiced by name, on the record, in front of strangers.
