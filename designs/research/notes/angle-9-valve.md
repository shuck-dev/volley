# Angle 9: Valve as the heaviest-scale evidence for open development

Reportorial notes for the section arguing Valve proves open-development principles scale. Valve is not an indie, not a hobby project, not a tool foundation. At every layer the customer touches, it operates closer to open development than to closed-shop industry practice.

## 1. Steam Workshop

- Launched October 13, 2011 with the Team Fortress 2 "Manniversary Update", initially for community-submitted TF2 cosmetic items. Extended to all games in early 2012, with Skyrim a flagship early adopter. ([TF Wiki: Steam Workshop](https://wiki.teamfortress.com/wiki/Steam_Workshop))
- By January 2015 Valve had paid out over $57 million to Workshop creators across TF2, Dota 2, and CS:GO. ([Engadget, 2015](https://www.engadget.com/2015-01-29-steam-workshop-payouts-thats-a-lot-of-hats.html); [Valve announcement, archived](https://store.steampowered.com/oldnews/15614)) Valve has not published a comprehensive update since.
- The CS2 Workshop publicly states a 25% revenue share for item creators on direct sales; allocation per contributor is at Valve's discretion. ([csgoskins.gg breakdown](https://csgoskins.gg/blog/how-much-do-csgo-skin-creators-earn))
- Workshop content is core, not cosmetic-to-the-business: third-party analysis estimated Valve grossed >$1B from CS2 case keys alone in 2024. ([Dexerto](https://www.dexerto.com/counter-strike-2/valve-made-over-1-billion-from-cs2-last-year-according-to-analysis-3303858/))

## 2. The mod lineage that became Valve's catalogue

Almost every Valve franchise except Half-Life itself originated outside Valve.

- **Counter-Strike** — Half-Life mod by Minh Le and Jess Cliffe, first beta June 1999. Valve acquired the IP and hired both in April 2000; retail release November 9, 2000. ([Wikipedia: Counter-Strike](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Counter-Strike_(video_game)))
- **Team Fortress** — 1996 Quake mod (Walker, Cook, Caughley); ported as Team Fortress Classic in 1999 then rebuilt as TF2. Robin Walker still works at Valve.
- **Portal** — grew from *Narbacular Drop*, the 2005 DigiPen senior project by Nuclear Monkey Software. Valve saw it at the DigiPen career fair; Newell hired the entire seven-person team. ([Wikipedia: Narbacular Drop](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Narbacular_Drop))
- **Garry's Mod** — Garry Newman's 2004 Half-Life 2 mod. Valve approached Newman to ship commercially November 29, 2006; reportedly handed him the full Source engine source in exchange for a 50% revenue share. >20M copies sold by 2021. ([Wikipedia: Garry's Mod](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Garry%27s_Mod))
- **Dota 2** — DotA was a Warcraft III mod lineage (Eul, Guinsoo, IceFrog). Valve hired IceFrog in October 2009 to lead Dota 2. ([Wikipedia: Dota](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dota))

## 3. The 2003 Half-Life 2 source leak

German hacker Axel Gembe breached Valve's network in 2003 (a `build` account with blank password); Source engine code leaked October 2, 2003 and an unfinished HL2 build October 7. Newell posted publicly on Halflife2.net asking the community for help tracking the hackers. The game shipped November 2004 to massive success; Valve subsequently released the Source SDK openly and a long-tail modding scene formed around it. ([Combine OverWiki](https://combineoverwiki.net/wiki/Half-Life_2_leak); [Kotaku](https://kotaku.com/that-time-a-german-hacker-leaked-half-life-2s-source-co-1737015166)) Useful counter-evidence to the "exposed source kills you" fear.

## 4. Proton and SteamOS

- Proton released August 21, 2018 as open-source compatibility tool built on Wine + DXVK. Initial 27 whitelisted titles; ProtonDB now lists tens of thousands. ([Wikipedia: Proton](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Proton_(software)); [GitHub: ValveSoftware/Proton](https://github.com/ValveSoftware/Proton))
- Steam Deck shipped February 25, 2022 on SteamOS (Arch-based Linux). Valve has not published official sales; IDC estimated ~3.7-4M units by Feb 2025. ([Wikipedia: Steam Deck](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Steam_Deck))
- Steam Hardware Survey: Linux sat ~1.0% pre-Deck, climbed to 1.38% (Dec 2022), 1.9% (2023), and 5.33% by March 2026 — the highest figure ever recorded. ([Steam Hardware Survey](https://store.steampowered.com/hwsurvey/Steam-Hardware-Software-Survey-Welcome-to-Steam); [Tom's Hardware](https://www.tomshardware.com/tech-industry/linux-usage-hits-an-all-time-high-in-steam-hardware-survey-and-amd-processors-continue-their-march-against-intel))

## 5. *Free to Play* (2014)

Valve-produced feature documentary following three Dota 2 pros at the first International. Premiered March 18, 2014 at the Castro Theatre; released free on Steam and YouTube the next day; >5.5M views within a week. ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Free_to_Play_(film)); [Engadget](https://www.engadget.com/2014-03-24-valves-dota-2-documentary-free-to-play-tallies-over-5-5-mil.html)) A AAA developer publishing a free, externally-distributed documentary about its game is itself a build-in-public artefact.

## 6. The 2012 Handbook for New Employees

Leaked April 2012. Self-described "flatland": no managers, "nobody reports to anybody else", desks on wheels. Became required reading in management-theory circles for years. ([PC Gamer](https://www.pcgamer.com/valve-employee-handbook-leaked-read-it-here/); [Hacker News](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=12157993))

The reality was messier. People Make Games' investigation, plus PC Gamer's reporting on ex-employee accounts, document a hidden hierarchy, stack-ranking that drove risk-aversion near review time, and diversity collapsing under peer review. ([PC Gamer: ex-Valve on internal politics](https://www.pcgamer.com/ex-valve-employee-describes-ruthless-industry-politics/); [PC Gamer: structure causes problems](https://www.pcgamer.com/valves-unusual-corporate-structure-causes-its-problems-report-suggests/)) Worth citing both — the cultural value of the document came from publishing the principles at all, then enduring the public audit.

## 7. Newell on openness

- LinuxCon NA, September 2013, opening line: *"It feels a little bit funny coming here and telling you guys that Linux and open source are the future of gaming, sort of like going to Rome and teaching Catholicism to the pope."* And: *"What we became convinced of is that Linux really is the future of gaming."* ([GamingOnLinux](https://www.gamingonlinux.com/2013/09/gabe-newell-at-linuxcon-2013-linux-is-the-future-of-gaming/); [GeekWire](https://www.geekwire.com/2013/valve-cofounder-gabe-newell-linux-future-gaming/); [video](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=rCGMiT0CQAI))
- The piracy line, 2011 (WTIA Tech NW / Cambridge Student): *"In general, we think there is a fundamental misconception about piracy. Piracy is almost always a service problem and not a pricing problem. If a pirate offers a product anywhere in the world, 24x7, purchasable from the convenience of your personal computer, and the legal provider says the product is region-locked, will come to your country 3 months after the U.S. release, and can only be purchased at a brick and mortar store, then the pirate's service is more valuable."* ([The Escapist](https://www.escapistmagazine.com/valves-gabe-newell-says-piracy-is-a-service-problem/); [GamesRadar](https://www.gamesradar.com/gabe-newell-piracy-issue-service-not-price/))

## Honest caveats

- Steam itself is proprietary and Valve takes 30% of sales.
- Source 1 SDK was open; Source 2 is not.
- The Handbook described an aspiration; ex-employee testimony shows the lived org was meaningfully more political and hierarchical.
- Valve is famously silent on internal projects (Half-Life 3, Citadel, etc).

The argument is not that Valve is fully open. It is that at every customer-facing layer — engine SDKs, mod-to-product pipelines, user-generated cosmetics, OS, runtime, even documentary filmmaking — Valve operates on principles closer to open development than its peers, and these are the principles that built the company into the largest PC games business on the planet.
