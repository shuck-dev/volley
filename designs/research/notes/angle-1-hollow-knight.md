# Angle 1: Hollow Knight Kickstarter as Audience Formation

Research notes. Cited facts and direct quotes; not prose.

## 1. Final campaign numbers (close, Dec 2014)

- Campaign ran 19 Nov 2014 to 19 Dec 2014 (30 days). [source: https://hollowknight.wiki/w/Hollow_Knight_Kickstarter]
- Goal: AU$35,000. [source: https://hollowknight.wiki/w/Hollow_Knight_Kickstarter]
- Final raised: AU$57,138 from 2,158 backers (Wikipedia cites "more than A$57,000"). [source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hollow_Knight] [source: https://www.kicktraq.com/projects/11662585/hollow-knight/]
- Approximately 163% of goal; average pledge approx AU$26. [source: https://www.kicktraq.com/projects/11662585/hollow-knight/]
- Hollow Knight wiki lists the gross at AU$58,893 (includes late-add/paypal tallies). Use the AU$57,138 Kickstarter-platform figure as primary. [source: https://hollowknight.wiki/w/Hollow_Knight_Kickstarter]
- Momentum: reached 10% of goal within 24 hours of launch. [source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ari_Gibson, via Source Gaming interview summary: https://sourcegaming.info/2025/04/09/straight-from-the-source-team-cherry/]

## 2. Pre-Kickstarter visibility

- Hungry Knight, Ludum Dare 27, released 26 Aug 2013 on Newgrounds. Team Cherry's first public appearance of the Knight character design. Initially scored 1/5. [source: https://hollowknight.wiki/w/Hungry_Knight] [source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hollow_Knight]
- A later Ludum Dare jam (theme "Beneath the Surface") was missed as a deadline, but the concept art they produced became the first imagery of Dirtmouth and Forgotten Crossroads. This directly seeded the Kickstarter pitch. [source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hollow_Knight]
- No substantial evidence of a long-running dev-blog or Tumblr presence before the campaign in search results; the Kickstarter page itself, plus the Ludum Dare / Newgrounds footprint, appear to be what prospective backers saw first. ## 3. Cadence of updates, during and after

- Could not get an authoritative live count from Kickstarter (fetch returned 403). One secondary search result listed "2 Updates, 76 Comments" on the public page, which clearly reflects consolidation/pruning; the wiki and fan accounts describe many update posts over years. [source: https://www.kickstarter.com/projects/11662585/hollow-knight, indirect]
- Post-campaign updates continued as a public dev-log. Updates described individual bestiary entries ("Dryya," "Ogrim/Dung Defender") with art and gifs. Many creatures introduced in updates were later cut, but the updates themselves functioned as serialised worldbuilding for backers. [source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hollow_Knight]
- First backer beta build went out September 2015, ~9 months after campaign close. [source: https://hollowknight.fandom.com/wiki/Hollow_Knight_Beta]
- After a ~6-year silence, Team Cherry posted a Kickstarter update in 2021 about Silksong codes. Backers' response: broadly supportive rather than angry. [source: https://www.gamespot.com/articles/first-hollow-knight-kickstarter-update-in-6-years-promises-backers-silksong-codes/1100-6534211/]

## 4. Backer language

- Could not verify specific Kickstarter backer comment quotes via search. [flag: the Kickstarter comments page itself is needed for primary quotes; fetch was blocked.]
- Indirect, reported sentiment: fans "popping into the comments section to tell Team Cherry to keep their heads up" during the long Silksong wait. [source: search result summary for https://www.kickstarter.com/projects/11662585/hollow-knight]
- Team Cherry on the quality of community-hired translators (later wave, but same mechanism): "They're people who had already played the game and were really passionate about it." — William Pellen. [source: https://sourcegaming.info/2025/04/09/straight-from-the-source-team-cherry/]
- Team Cherry on how community work got done: "A lot of that has actually been through the help of fans and through the community coming to us." — Ari Gibson. [source: https://sourcegaming.info/2025/04/09/straight-from-the-source-team-cherry/]

## 5. Team Cherry on campaign-as-relationship

- No direct quote found where Team Cherry frames Kickstarter explicitly as "audience formation over fundraising." Closest statements:
  - On budget sufficiency and community dependence: "That budget did sustain us but it was because of our specific situation." — Ari Gibson. [source: https://sourcegaming.info/2025/04/09/straight-from-the-source-team-cherry/]
  - On scope growth through backer involvement: initial Kickstarter funded "a Metroidvania that was very small," and subsequent funding "allowed the game to get bigger again." — Ari Gibson. [source: https://sourcegaming.info/2025/04/09/straight-from-the-source-team-cherry/]
  - On player relationship as design goal: "people give these really detailed accounts of their own path and they talk about how it feels like their own." — Ari Gibson. [source: https://www.mcvuk.com/when-we-made-hollow-knight/]
- [flag: no interview found where Team Cherry states in so many words that the campaign was about building a community. Evidence is circumstantial: backers funded the bestiary updates, backers made NPCs, backers translated the game.]

## 6. Sales, launch vs later

- PC launch: 24 Feb 2017. [source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hollow_Knight]
- Nov 2017: over 500,000 copies. [source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hollow_Knight]
- 11 Jun 2018: over 1,000,000 on PC (one day before Switch release). [source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hollow_Knight]
- Two weeks post-Switch: over 250,000 on Switch alone. [source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hollow_Knight]
- Jul 2018: over 1.2 million total. Feb 2019: over 2.8 million. [source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hollow_Knight]
- Aug 2025: "more than 15 million copies" ahead of Silksong. [source: https://www.nintendolife.com/news/2025/08/hollow-knight-has-now-sold-almost-15-million-copies]
- Ratio worth noting: 2,158 Kickstarter backers seeded an audience that reached ~500k within 9 months of launch and 15M eight years on.

## 7. Quotes that land the point

- Ari Gibson on community NPCs/translation/content: "A lot of that has actually been through the help of fans and through the community coming to us." [source: https://sourcegaming.info/2025/04/09/straight-from-the-source-team-cherry/]
- William Pellen on community translators being pre-existing fans: "They're people who had already played the game and were really passionate about it." [source: https://sourcegaming.info/2025/04/09/straight-from-the-source-team-cherry/]
- Ari Gibson on the player's sense of ownership: players feel "the journey they had was their own, had its own shape." [source: https://www.mcvuk.com/when-we-made-hollow-knight/]
