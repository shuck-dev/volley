# Volley Vendetta - Pricing & Sales Strategy

## Launch Price

**$7.99 / £6.49**

### Rationale

- **Genre comps:** Cozy idle desktop games sit in the $5–$10 band. Cookie Clicker is $4.99, but Volley Vendetta has a genuine narrative hook (The Break) and a prestige loop, which justify the higher end of that range.
- **Audience:** Casual players who run it in the background are price-sensitive. Anything above $9.99 creates friction at the point of impulse buy. $7.99 is the sweet spot, below the psychological $10 barrier, above "feels cheap."
- **v1 scope:** v1 ships with the full core loop, 3–5 partners, milestones, prestige, and The Break twist. That's a complete, polished package, not a thin idle clicker.
- **Steam context:** Steam's discovery algorithm rewards strong launch week sales volume. A lower price gets more wishlists converting, which feeds visibility.

### The case for $9.99

If The Break lands as a genuinely memorable moment and beta reviews reflect that, $9.99 is defensible and better for overall revenue per unit. Watch the itch.io beta response before committing.

---

## Sales Strategy

**Never discount at launch.** Full price only during launch week. Launch week sales feed review count and Steam ranking. Discounting immediately signals low confidence and trains buyers to wait.

| Timing | Discount | Price | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Launch week | 0% | $7.99 | Never discount here |
| 30–90 days post-launch | 20–25% | ~$5.99 | Re-engages wishlists, second visibility bump |
| Seasonal sales | 30–40% | ~$4.79 | Steam Next Fest, Summer, Winter, where volume happens |
| 12+ months in | 50% | $3.99 | Use sparingly; anchors expectations once used |

**Never go below 50%.** $3.99 is the floor where the game still feels like it has value.

---

## Pairing Sales with Content Updates

Pair sales with content drops (new partner, post-launch feature). The update gives press and social something to talk about; the sale converts the audience that comes from it. The two together punch harder than either alone.

---

## What to Avoid

- **Free-to-play:** the design has no monetisation hooks ("pinch points, not paywalls"). Retooling for IAP would undermine the tone entirely.
- **Early launch discounts:** signals low confidence and trains the audience to wait for sales.
- **Going below 50%:** perception drops into asset-flip territory at this price point.
