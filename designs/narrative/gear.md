# Gear

Gear hangs on the rack at the edge of the court. Each piece is a thing in the world; each piece has a place it goes on the body when the protagonist takes it up to wear into the rally.

Friendship lifts the gear into place. The protagonist channels what the meetings between beings have made; the gear rises to the body and settles where it belongs. The visible work of putting on equipment is the visible work of being met. The gear stays on the rack until friendship reaches for it.

The protagonist can channel only as much as their meetings have built up. Capacity is the accumulated weight of having been met across rallies. It grows as the protagonist trains and meets. A new piece arrives in the world when the meetings behind it have done the work to support it.

When the protagonist reaches for a piece that asks more of friendship than the channel can give yet, the piece stays on its hook. The protagonist says, in their own voice, "I haven't grown into this yet," and returns to what their shoulders already know how to carry. The piece will lift when the meetings have grown into it.
