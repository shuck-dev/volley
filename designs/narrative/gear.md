# Gear

The wristband. The shoes. The light brace at the knee. Each piece sits on its hook in the workshop until the morning a friend hands it down, and then it goes on the body the way a cat settles on a lap, heavy and familiar at once. The friend behind the counter calls the protagonist's name across the court, and the equipment carries that calling. What looks from the bench like a player getting dressed is the slow, visible work of being known. The weight is the affection. Lift comes from there.

What the protagonist can carry on any morning is what the meetings have so far set down in them. A name called across the court last week sits in the shoulder today; a partner's tilt, held the right beat, sits in the wrist. They are members of each other already, the protagonist and the friend behind the counter and every partner who has stood across the rally; the only question is how much of it the protagonist has yet learned to feel. The garden hands the equipment over at the pace the meetings have earned. A piece arrives the day the friendship behind it has done the work to arrive.

There are mornings the protagonist reaches for a piece that has arrived ahead of them. They lift it down, feel its weight, and set it back on the hook. "I haven't grown into this yet," they say, with the same care they would give a friend whose hand they were declining for the first dance of the evening. The piece keeps its place. The friendship it answers to keeps its pace. The protagonist returns to what their shoulders already know how to hold, and trains the rally that brought them this far.
