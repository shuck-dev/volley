# Narrative discipline

How the prose, scenes, and dialogue of Volley get made. Not what the story is: that lives in [`outline.md`](outline.md). This is the craft the writing aims for, line by line.

The prose voice lives in [`../../ai/STYLE.md`](../../ai/STYLE.md). The world canon sits in [`../art/bible.md`](../art/bible.md). The protagonist's interior is in [`../characters/protagonist.md`](../characters/protagonist.md). The structural spine is in [`../concept/`](../concept/). Read those first; this doc tells you how to write inside them.

Each principle below is one line. Applications and worked examples live in actual scene drafts, not here. This doc is a quick reference; expect it to sharpen and shift as the story does.

## Names and canon

- **Describe before naming.** Open with what the place feels like; introduce the canon proper noun (Construction, Reality, Reconstruction) only after the reader has the picture. Leading with the canon name in summary prose spoils the structure.
- **Physical canon lives in the character profile.** Outline and discipline summaries do not restate the protagonist's physical detail; point at `designs/characters/protagonist.md` when role-shape needs context.

## Scene construction

- **Layer first, prune second.** A scene runs more than the player will touch in one visit; the unengaged pieces are the room.
- **Place the nouns before the people.** Geography first; the reader assembles the room as fast as the prose lays it down.

## Dialogue

- **Reality speaks plainer.** Half-finished sentences, the thing said while looking at something else, long pauses the prose holds instead of fills.
- **Memory arrives sideways.** Characters mention the shopkeeper without explaining; the player assembles them from asymmetric particulars.
- **Canon lands as action.** A piece of world reaches the player as something a character does or as something the world shows; never as told flavour.

## Tone modulation

- **Slip and resettle.** One beat of meta-crack, one line carrying the weight, one beat of resettle. A crackle that runs two paragraphs has overcommitted.
- **The break runs cold and short.** Sentences shrink. Adjectives drop. Light becomes light, sound becomes sound.
- **Dread as floor, not adjective.** Part 2's temperature is low by default; warmth lands brighter against it. Don't paint dread on top.

## Memory surfacings

- **Present-tense surfacing in the rally.** Memory arrives as a half-second image in present tense and fades on the next hit.
- **Specificity over warmth.** Surfacings come from particular moments: a sleeve, a laugh, the way a racquet was handed back. Generalities reach for meaning before the picture earns it.

## Cracks at the line level

- **A meta-crack in dialogue.** A line that almost-acknowledges the construct, stays in-fiction, leaves the player room to read it kindly.
- **A tonal crack in description.** Plain prose, two sentences, no character noticing. The player notices.
- **The cumulative load.** One crack is a flourish; a dozen across an act is the world failing to reconcile. Calibrate so the player can still reach for an in-fiction explanation.

## Pacing rhythms

- **Push when the moment pushes.** Short sentences in a stack for the break, the cliff walk, the recognition.
- **Settle when the moment settles.** Longer paragraphs that breathe for kitchen tables, quiet coach lines, the credits rally.

## What the writing reaches for

- **Endings land on a particular.** Close on the loaded image; the reader carries the meaning out the door.
- **Themes live in what happens.** The rally, the stall, the cliff, the call. The prose lets the theme arrive without naming it.
- **Dialogue plays oblique to the theme.** Characters talk about the weather, the toast, the bus. The theme rides underneath.
