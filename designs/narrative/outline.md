# Volley! narrative outline

The structural spine of the game in four acts and a postgame. Story prose lives in [`story.md`](story.md). World and visual canon live in [`../art/bible.md`](../art/bible.md). Per-character portraits live in [`../characters/`](../characters/). Mechanics live in the system docs and are not repeated here.

This rewrite makes canon shifts the supporting docs do not yet reflect; the cascade is named at the foot of the doc.

## Act I. Construction

The protagonist trains in the garden. They are in their twenties, in the middle stretch of a life searching for a purpose, and the purpose has settled on a single number: the world volley record. They have hope and vigour and nothing else. No racquet, no partner, no equipment. They start with what is there. A ball from the bushes, the wall of the house, the sound of it coming back. The wall does not count toward the record (the count needs consecutive hits on a racquet) but the bouncing builds their soul, and soul is what lets a racquet hold the count later.

When the soul has gathered, the protagonist imbues their first racquet at the stall against the inside wall. The shopkeep leans on the counter on the far side of the play, watching, glad to be there. They are one of the protagonist's best friends. The racquet allows for consistent hits and the first small lift; the ball begins to speed up. The rally has started.

The shop fills out. The stall acquires items the shopkeep has scavenged from home. A catalogue arrives later, holding bigger things: a volley ticker, court furniture, decorations, dog helpers that fetch a ball when it rolls out of bounds. The shopkeep's younger sister, the tinkerer, takes a corner of the stall as her workshop and quietly upgrades whatever the protagonist brings her, finished pieces landing on a done tray with a soft chime. Partners arrive over the months. Martha first, the cashier at the newsagent the protagonist passed every morning of a childhood spent here. The rest come from the same kind of place. Each partner teaches one piece of the rally; the count climbs.

At the top of the ladder sits another friend, painted at championship scale, present in Construction at the final venue. The protagonist climbs toward them. At the back of the garden, behind the stall and the tree, a gate sits in the wall, locked. The protagonist never opens it.

## Act II. Break

The protagonist is fully kitted and the record is in sight. The want is no longer joy. It has become need, then obsession, then a thing the protagonist is too far in to put down. Across the climb small things start to arrive wrong. A light at the corner of the court cools and does not warm. A partner's tilt is held a beat too long. A music cue skips. A UI element blinks the wrong colour. The protagonist does not name it.

The championship arrives. The protagonist meets the friend at the final venue and wins. The number lands. Everything slows. Sounds the protagonist has been not hearing get louder, then unbearable. They clutch their face and black out.

They wake in the garden. The colour has gone. The stall is empty. The family home behind the wall is run down. There is no one here. This is the garden as it actually is, and has been.

They leave the garden and walk through the town for the first time in years. They are heavier in the chest than they were. They remember they do not live here anymore. The shop on the high street is closed, a slip wedged in the door a week old. Inside is Zach's family shop, and Zach's younger sister is at the counter, crying. Zach is gone. No one has heard from him. In the shape of the conversation, in the photographs on the wall behind her, something else surfaces: a friend the protagonist used to play with, who is also not here, and has not been for years. The protagonist's attention slides past it. The wall holds.

The garden is back when the protagonist returns to it, lit again, but the stall is empty and the count does not bloom on the apex of a return. The wall has come down once and will not go back up. The warm centre is hollowed out. The rallies render the absence.

## Act III. Reconstruction

To draft. The act takes place primarily in Reality (the world). The protagonist gains the ability to travel between Construction and Reality at will; the record falls to the side; the work becomes finding Zach. Soul powers turn out to be partly real, not only a Construction conceit. Items carry from Reality into Construction; a memorable object invokes a memory, and each memory narrows where Zach might be.

## Ending

To draft. The final memory yields a key. The key unlocks the gate at the back of the garden. Through the gate is the cliff, and on the memorial bench dedicated to the friend who is not here, Zach is sitting.

## Postgame

To draft. Zach is found and now shares Construction with the protagonist, knowing what it is. The game continues. New items, new venues, the same rally with the centre filled.

1. **Soul replaces friendship.** Rename `friendship.md` to `soul.md`; rewrite the body. Update `../art/bible.md` (the apex-return beat in §17, glossary mentions), `../characters/protagonist.md`, `../characters/partners.md`, and any other doc using *friendship* as the mechanic noun.
2. **Reality moves to 2014; Construction holds the 90s.** Update `../art/bible.md` §2 (setting and period) and §1 (the phone hardware framing). The protagonist is mid-40s in Reality, rendered in their twenties in Construction.
3. **The phone-call resolution is retired.** Remove the unnamed-number mechanic from `../art/bible.md` §1, §9, §10, glossary; from `../characters/protagonist.md` (Part 2 and "the cliff and the call"); from `../characters/shopkeeper.md`. The ending stands on the gate, the cliff, and Zach on the bench.
4. **Zach and Stephanie are placeholder names.** Resolve before the doc locks. `../characters/shopkeeper.md` uses Zach; `../characters/sister.md` does not name the sister yet.
