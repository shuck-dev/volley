# Friendship

Friendship is what a committed rally is doing. It rises with each return, holds the ball aloft as long as the exchange holds, and the rally counter reads how present it has become.

The spirit of the volley is friendship in flight. Two names, one quality: "friendship" is what it is, "spirit" is how the rally feels for it. Other languages name the same shape elsewhere: a fighting spirit, the spirit of a place. Volley names it friendship because friendship is the shape it takes here.

A miss ends the rally; friendship recedes with it. The ball falls under gravity and rolls until it rests on the venue floor.

A player calls friendship alone. Partners deepen its presence. High-count rallies run hotter, and the ball reads warmer for it: a charge in its glow, weight in its sound. After a miss the rally counter resets and the ball returns to plain. Items that extend or revive rallies read in-fiction as gestures of devotion.
