# Notes on writing Volley

This is how Volley wants to be written. Not as rules, as the patterns that hold up when the prose is doing what the game asks of it. The story lives in [`outline.md`](outline.md). The world is in [`../art/bible.md`](../art/bible.md). The protagonist's interior is in [`../characters/protagonist.md`](../characters/protagonist.md). The structural spine is in [`../concept/`](../concept/). Read those first; this is the companion that helps the rest land.

The principles are short on purpose. Each one points at a pattern the writer recognises in the moment; the worked example lives in the scene you are writing, not here. Expect them to sharpen and shift as the story does.

## Scene construction

- **Layer first, prune second.** A scene runs more than the player will touch in one visit. The pieces they do not engage with are the room they walked into.
- **Place the nouns before the people.** Build the geometry first; the reader assembles a room as fast as the prose lays it down.

## Dialogue

- **Reality speaks plainer.** Half-finished sentences. The thing said while looking at something else. Long pauses the prose holds instead of fills.
- **Memory arrives sideways.** Characters mention the shopkeeper without explaining; the player assembles them from asymmetric particulars.
- **Canon lands as action.** Anything that needs to reach the player reaches it as something a character does, or as something the world shows. Never as flavour text.

## Tone modulation

- **Slip and resettle.** One beat of meta-crack, one line carrying the weight, one beat back to the rally. If the crackle runs two paragraphs, the prose has stopped pretending.
- **The break runs cold and short.** Sentences shrink. Adjectives drop. Light becomes light, sound becomes sound.
- **Dread as floor, not adjective.** Part 2's temperature is low by default; warmth lands brighter against it. Don't paint dread on top of warmth that's already there.

## Memory surfacings

- **Present-tense surfacing in the rally.** Memory arrives as a half-second image in present tense and fades on the next hit. Past tense narrates; present tense surfaces.
- **Specificity over warmth.** Surfacings come from particular moments: a sleeve, a laugh, the way a racquet was handed back. Generalities reach for the meaning before the picture has earned it.

## Cracks at the line level

- **A meta-crack in dialogue.** A line that almost-acknowledges the construct. Stays in-fiction; leaves the player room to read it kindly.
- **A tonal crack in description.** Plain prose, two sentences, no character noticing. The player does.
- **The cumulative load.** One crack is a flourish; a dozen across an act is the world failing to reconcile. Calibrate so the player can still reach for an in-fiction explanation.

## Pacing rhythms

- **Push when the moment pushes.** Short sentences in a stack: for the break, the cliff walk, the recognition.
- **Settle when the moment settles.** Longer paragraphs that breathe: for kitchen tables, quiet coach lines, the credits rally.

## What the writing reaches for

- **Endings land on a particular.** Close on the loaded image; the reader carries the meaning out the door with them.
- **Themes live in what happens.** The rally. The stall. The cliff. The call. The prose lets the theme arrive without naming it.
- **Dialogue plays oblique to the theme.** Characters talk about the weather, the toast, the bus. The theme rides underneath.
