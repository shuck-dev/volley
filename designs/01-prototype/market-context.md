# Volley! Market Visual Context

Where the game sits visually among its neighbours, and why the art direction matters.

---

## What Idle Games Look Like

Most idle games look like dashboards.

- Large numbers, progress bars, upgrade sidebars, inventory grids
- Cookie Clicker: static sprite with a list (96% positive, 89K reviews)
- NGU Idle, Melvor Idle: spreadsheets with game-themed skin
- Clicker Heroes: simple Flash-era cartoon art
- AdVenture Capitalist: flat-design cartoon with bold primary colours
- The visual convention: numbers first, atmosphere optional

The few that break this pattern:

- **Text-only**
  - A Dark Room, Universal Paperclips, Candy Box, Kittens Game, Antimatter Dimensions
  - No art at all, atmosphere from writing and pacing
  - Proves idle games can compel with zero visual investment
- **Retro pixel**
  - Idle Slayer: pixel art auto-runner
  - Leaf Blower Revolution: colourful pixel art with playful palette
  - The most common visual approach for idle games on Steam
- **Cozy illustration**
  - Cats and Soup, Tsuki Adventure: soft palettes, rounded shapes, nature/domestic themes
  - Farmer Against Potatoes: cartoon 2D with exaggerated proportions
  - Neko Atsume: simple, minimal art with a check-in-occasionally play pattern and hidden emotional warmth
- **Minimalist but intentional**
  - Gnorp Apologue: almost scientific-diagram aesthetic, sparse with occasional colour accents (critically acclaimed, 2023)
  - SPACEPLAN: retrofuturist UI with analog dials and CRT glow (BAFTA-nominated)
- **Mobile polish**
  - Eggs Inc: clean 3D low-poly farm
  - Cell to Singularity: dark UI with particle effects, science-documentary tone
- **Editorial metaphor**
  - Godville: newspaper/journal visual framing with illustrated diary entries

## What Desktop/Widget Games Look Like

Games that live on your desktop while you work.

- **Toolbar/window games**
  - Rusty's Retirement: pixel-art farming in a translucent toolbar strip (97% positive, 14K reviews, 550K+ copies)
    - The closest format comparison to Volley!
    - Warm, earthy pixel palette, stays out of the way
  - Desktopia: tiny villagers and buildings rendered directly on your desktop, cozy low-poly pixel
  - Desktop Meadow: birds and butterflies as watercolour-inspired sprites
    - Small, quiet, ambient desktop presence with considered art
    - Closer in spirit to Volley! than most desktop games
- **Desktop pets/companions**
  - Desktop Goose: hand-drawn cartoon goose that drags memes onto your screen
  - Shimeji: anime-style pixel mascots that climb windows
  - Tabagotchi: Tamagotchi-style pixel pet in a browser tab
- **Common traits**
  - Pixel art dominates
  - Deliberately lo-fi or cute
  - Personality through animation behaviour, not art fidelity
  - Minimal visual footprint, designed to not compete with your work

## What Pong-likes Look Like

- Pong-likes exist as action games (Pong Quest, Bit Ball), not idle games
- Sports idle games (Idle Soccer Tycoon, Boxing Clicker) use generic mobile UI with sport-themed icons
  - No real-time gameplay visualisation
- No precedent for an idle game with real-time animated sport as its visual centre

## Where Volley! Sits

Volley! does not look like any of these.

- **Not a dashboard**
  - The game is a live scene, not a menu with numbers
  - The player watches paddles volley a ball, not a progress bar filling
- **Not pixel art**
  - Retro pixel is the default visual language for Steam idle and desktop games
  - Volley! is hand-drawn illustration, not pixel art
- **Not lo-fi or ironic**
  - Desktop games lean into meme aesthetics and minimal art
  - Volley! takes itself seriously: warm and earnest, not deliberately rough
- **Not mobile polish**
  - Hand-drawn and characterful, not 3D rendered or particle-heavy
- **Not generic cozy**
  - The cozy subgenre defaults to rounded shapes, pastel animals, nature themes
  - Volley! has cozy warmth but with specificity, personality, and a dual-world structure underneath
- **Closest to Rusty's Retirement in format**
  - Small persistent window, plays while you work
  - Rusty's is pixel art and lo-fi, focused on mechanical satisfaction
  - Volley! layers considered art direction, expressive animation, and narrative on top of the same "play while you work" format

Closest visual neighbours (outside the idle space):

- **Omori**: dual-world, hand-drawn, emotional
- **Cuphead**: committed hand-drawn identity across every element
- **Ranking of Kings**: simple characters with enormous expression through animation
- None of these are idle, none live on your desktop

## Why This Matters for the Artist

- There is no established visual language for "idle desktop pong game with emotional depth"
- The artist is defining what this looks like, not iterating on a convention
- The risk is not looking too similar to competitors; there is nothing visually close to compete with
- The risk is looking too generic
  - Defaulting to pixel art or cozy illustration because those are the known paths
  - Neither fits the game's emotional range
- The commandments (Intentional, Specific, Distinct, Diegetic, Breathing) keep the style from drifting toward convention
