# Volley! Artist Brief: Style Guide

---

## The Game

An idle pong game on your desktop with a hidden story about grief disguised as a sports game.

A small window, always there, gently bouncing away while you work. Two characters volley a ball back and forth in a small arena. The player controls one; the other is a partner. You train, buy items, recruit partners, and inch toward the world record one bounce at a time.

But the paddles are people. The main character is an ordinary person, somewhere in Britain, who lost their best friend and does not know how to deal with it. So they built a world. The arena, the partners who play alongside them, the items they collect: none of it is real. It is a fantasy the main character retreated into because reality was too much.

The people who appear as partners are based on real people from the main character's life. People who were kind to them, people who tried to help, people they remember. In the constructed world they are warm and friendly. In reality they are more complicated than that.

## Two Worlds

The art must support two visual worlds.

- **The Constructed World**: what the player sees first
  - This is not a real place; it is a fantasy built by the main character
  - The arena, the partners, the items: all projections of something real
  - Should feel warm, held, safe
  - Like someone needed things to be okay and built a place where they could be
  - The warmth is genuine, but it is also protection
- **The Real World**: what is underneath
  - An ordinary town, familiar and difficult
  - A place someone grew up and lost someone in, and everything there reminds them of it
  - Not ugly or hostile; just a place that holds things the constructed world was built to cover
  - The constructed world is a simplification of this place; the real world is what was there first

The shift between them should feel like waking up, not like changing the channel. How the two worlds relate visually is an open question for the artist to explore. The feeling matters more than the technique.

## The Commandments

Five principles that govern every piece of art. These are not style rules; they are qualities.

- **Intentional**: every mark, colour, and placement is a choice
- **Specific**: this object, this space, this person; never generic
- **Distinct**: communicates through shape and colour; unmistakable at any size
- **Diegetic**: part of the world, not on top of it
- **Breathing**: feels alive

## Style Direction

The style should feel hand-made and personal. Characters that are simple but expressive. Colour that is warm and considered in the constructed world, quieter and more natural in the real world. Shapes that read clearly at any size. A world that feels like someone drew it with care, not generated or assembled.

The mood boards show the territory, not the destination. The references are ingredients, not a recipe. You have creative freedom to mix, reinterpret, and push in directions the mood boards do not cover. We are looking for your take on this, not a composite of the references.

## Constraints

- Expressive but efficient to produce; the style should support iteration
- The style must work for both visual worlds

## The Game in Play

A dev build is available at: https://j-melon.itch.io/volley-vendetta

In the current build: a paddle and a wall volley a ball back and forth. A HUD tracks volleys and currency. There is a shop for buying items. The paddle can play on its own when the player steps away.

The build uses placeholder art throughout. The square aspect ratio is not final.

## Deliverables

Two concept pieces showing both worlds:

1. **The constructed world**: the arena during play
   - Two characters with something to hit a ball with
   - The ball
   - The arena location

2. **The real world**: a memory
   - A quiet moment between the main character and a partner in an ordinary place
   - Fly on the wall; we are seeing something real that the constructed world was built from
   - Same visual language, different feeling

Both pieces should give a clear feel for line, shape, colour, and tone. They do not need to be final or polished; they need to show a direction and the shift between the two worlds.

Include any supplementary info or design as you see fit!
