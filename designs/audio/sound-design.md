# Sound design

Everything that is not the score. The rally, the counter, the stall, the shop, the menu. The world's foreground and the player's interface.

## The rally

The ball is the loudest thing the player hears most of the time. The hit is one sound; the bounce is another; the catch (when the ball goes home) is another. Pitch and timbre vary by venue: the garden plays differently than the championship court.

The rally is the metronome. Every other sound design choice has to sit politely under it.

## The counter

The number on the wall ticks. The tick is small, dry, almost not there. The player notices it the way a person notices a clock at three in the morning: only when the room is quiet.

## The stall

The shopkeeper's stall is the warmest pocket of sound in Construction. Wood, paper, the small bell when someone leans on the counter. No music; the stall is heard against the score, not on top of it.

## The sister's bench

The bench is mechanical: small clinks, a screwdriver, the hiss of a hand-vice. The sister's work is what the player hears when nothing is being said.

## UI

Menus are quiet. A tap is a soft click; a confirm is the same click with a small wooden resonance behind it. Errors are not buzzers; they are the absence of a confirm.

## The shop

The shop is a separate room from the rally. The score steps back; the shop's own room tone takes over. Coins, paper bags, the small sounds of items being lifted off a shelf.

## What we are not building

No voice barks. No dramatic stingers tied to gameplay events; the score is the score and the rally is the rally, and neither needs help to land. No randomised one-shots that aim for variety through volume; variety comes from the venue, not from the playback layer.

## Owners

Sound design is its own discipline once the team grows. Currently it sits with the engine team. The composer ([Audio Bible](bible.md)) does not write sound design; the bible's "what we are not" rule applies here as well.
