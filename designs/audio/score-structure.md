# Score structure

How the bible's two voices live in the music: the themes, where they appear, how they meet.

## Two themes

There are two leitmotifs the player learns. Construction has one and Reality has the other. The two are written to be related at the level of interval or shape, not melody, so that when they begin to meet in Reconstruction the player feels the connection before they can name it.

## Where they appear

**Construction.** The Construction theme runs the venues. Each venue gets a treatment: same melodic material, different palette, different tempo, different instrument carrying the line. The garden gets one face; the championship gets the off-the-chain face. The piano reduction is the bone the venue treatments hang from.

**The break.** The Construction theme stops mid-phrase. The first time the player hears music after the break is silence and weather.

**Reality.** The Reality theme arrives quietly and acoustically. It is not a fanfare; it is what the player hears in a room. It does not contend with the Construction theme; it does not know about it yet.

**Reconstruction.** The two themes begin to share material. The first crossing is small: one phrase from the Construction theme appears under a Reality instrumentation, or the inverse. Subsequent crossings are larger. By the cliff and the call, the two are inseparable. The reconciliation is in the score because it is in the protagonist.

**The championship final.** The Champ scene is the only place in Construction that hears Reality leak in early. The leak is what tells the player something is wrong before the prose does.

**Postgame.** Both themes coexist freely. The player can hear either, depending on where they are.

## What we are not writing

Not stingers tied to gameplay events. Not a victory cue every round. Not a separate menu theme that lives outside the world. The score is the world's, not the game-shell's.

## Owners

The composer holds the themes. The engine team holds the seam between composition and runtime ([Tech Pipeline](tech-pipeline.md)). Sound design ([Sound Design](sound-design.md)) is a separate discipline; its work is not the leitmotifs.
