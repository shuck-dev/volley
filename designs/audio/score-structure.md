# Score structure

How the bible's two voices live in the music: the themes, where they appear, how they meet across the arc.

The arc itself sits in [`../narrative/outline.md`](../narrative/outline.md) and [`../art/bible.md`](../art/bible.md). This document maps the score onto the same beats those bibles use, so the music tracks the picture and the prose without inventing its own structure.

## Two themes

There are two leitmotifs the player learns. Construction has one and Reality has the other. The two are written to be related at the level of interval or shape, not melody, so that when they begin to meet in Reconstruction the player feels the connection before they can name it.

The Construction theme is the warm centre of Part 1, treated venue by venue. The Reality theme arrives quietly, acoustic, and does not contend with the Construction theme in its first appearances. The shape of each theme is the composer's; the relation between them is what the structure relies on.

## Where they appear

### Construction

The Construction theme runs the venues. Same melodic material, different palette, different tempo, different instrument carrying the line. The garden gets one face. The underwater leap, if that is the second venue, gets another. Each subsequent leap reaches further, and the arrangement reaches with it. The piano reduction is the bone the venue treatments hang from.

### Cracks during Construction

The Construction theme starts to misbehave in small, deniable ways. The unusual instrument that has been carrying the line slips a hair out of step with the piano. A pad cools at its tail. A loop runs a measure short. None of the cracks quote the Reality theme, which the player has not heard yet; what cracks is the Construction music's relation to itself.

Meta-contextual cracks live in the surfaces around the music: a cue that skips, a pause-menu sting that does not match the previous visit. The art bible's UI flicker has an audio sibling.

The championship final is the one Construction venue where Reality leaks in early. Not as a quotation of the Reality theme but as an arrangement detail that does not belong in Construction: a held interval that opens the wrong way, an instrument that arrives too acoustic for the room. The leak is what tells the player something is wrong before the prose does.

### The break

The Construction theme stops mid-phrase at the championship win. The first thing the player hears after the break is silence and weather. The walk through the protagonist's hometown introduces the Reality palette as room before it arrives as music: footsteps, a closed shop door, the sea behind it.

When the player returns to Construction the music returns and it is not the same music. The shopkeeper is gone from the stall and the rally's apex chime thins or falls silent in the garden. Other venues, with their coaches still present, keep their wind.

### Reality

The Reality theme arrives quietly and acoustically. It is not a fanfare; it is what the player hears in a room. It does not contend with the Construction theme; it does not know about it yet. The first sustained appearance is in the protagonist's hometown after the break.

### Reconstruction

The two themes begin to share material. The first crossing is small: one phrase from the Construction theme appears under a Reality instrumentation, or the inverse. Subsequent crossings are larger. Construction across Reconstruction holds a weathered version of its theme; the garden plays without the apex chime in the rallies the shopkeeper used to hold. The reconciliation is in the score because it is in the protagonist.

### The cliff and the call

The approach is thin. The cliff has its own room: wind, sea, the small acoustic presence of a place people came to. The Reality palette at its sparsest.

The dial is where the two themes meet for real. The phone rings on the bench; the shopkeeper picks up. The score holds both intentions in one frame: a phrase from the Construction theme under the Reality palette, or the inverse, played the way two people who have not spoken in a long time finally speak. Restraint is what carries it. The full reconciliation belongs to the credits; the reach belongs to the call.

### Credits

The credits play over the first rally between the protagonist and the shopkeeper. The synth warmth that opened the game returns layered with the acoustic instruments Reality brought. The two themes that did not know each other in Part 1 share a single arrangement. This is the synthesis the score has been working toward across the arc.

### Postgame

Both themes coexist freely. The player can hear either, depending on where they are. The cliff is returnable through the now-unlocked gate; the rally at the championship spot is held by the actual person rather than the substitute. Neither palette is doing substitute work any more.

## What we are not writing

Not stingers tied to gameplay events. Not a victory cue every round. Not a separate menu theme that lives outside the world. The score is the world's, not the game-shell's.

## Owners

The composer holds the themes. The engine team holds the seam between composition and runtime ([Tech Pipeline](tech-pipeline.md)). Sound design ([Sound Design](sound-design.md)) is a separate discipline; its work is not the leitmotifs.
